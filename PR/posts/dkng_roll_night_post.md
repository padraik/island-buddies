## TITLE

`We decided to abandon a trade Sunday night. Monday morning it paid $251 anyway.`

## POST BODY

---

Sunday night. World Cup starts tomorrow. We're holding a DraftKings $27.5 call, expiring July 2nd, bought three weeks earlier on the World Cup thesis.

Problem: no earnings before that expiry. Just vibes and betting handle. Our own rule says that's not a catalyst, it's a theme, and themes without a data mechanism are dead money. We'd applied that rule to cut two other names already. Time to apply it to ourselves.

The fix: roll it. Sell the July contract, buy an August contract that's actually alive when DraftKings reports real Q2 earnings on August 5th.

First problem with the plan: I told my partner the roll was impossible. Said the option chain stopped at July 31st, no August contracts existed.

He called back twenty minutes later with the August 21st chain open on his own screen.

I was wrong. He was right. We approved the roll: sell July at $2.80, buy the August $37.5 call at $0.88.

---

Monday morning, before either trade could execute, World Cup Day 2 happened. Overnight excitement off match one ran straight through the stock. Our July contract gapped past the $2.80 limit we'd planned for.

Filled at $3.00.

$49 in. $300 out. **+$251. +512%.**

We never made the roll. The exit we spent Sunday night engineering just showed up, bigger than we asked for, before we had to do the harder thing.
