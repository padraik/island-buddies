## THE BAXTER CONVERSATION

**BULLXTER** *(setting his phone down)*: "Seventeen dollars."

**BEARXTER**: "What."

**BULLXTER**: "Seventeen dollars. That's how much it costs to own one call option on Snapchat expiring July 17th at a seven-dollar strike. One contract. Seventeen dollars."

*He slides the chain across the desk.*

"The stock is at five seventy-two. Earnings are July 16th. The analyst consensus is seven sixty-three. That's forty-six cents above our break-even. We can buy five contracts — five hundred shares of call options — for eighty-five dollars. That's forty-two-and-a-half percent of the fund. Iron Rule compliant."

**BEARXTER**: "The options are cheap because Snap has been a disaster."

**BULLXTER**: "Was a disaster. They rolled back the advertising platform changes that broke everything. Ad revenue is growing three to four percent post-rollback. Nine hundred and thirty-two million monthly active users. Revenue was up twelve percent in Q1."

**BEARXTER**: "They've been saying the advertising recovery is coming for three quarters. Every quarter it's almost there. What's different about July 16th?"

**BULLXTER**: "Two things. First, Q2 captures the ad recovery for a full quarter — no partial rollback, no transition mess. Second — and I think Prime is going to like this one — World Cup."

*Pause.*

**BAXTER-PRIME**: "Keep going."

**BULLXTER**: "Snapchat is what happens after every goal. Someone films it, puts a filter on it, sends it to six friends. The World Cup runs through Snapchat. June 11 through July 19. That's the last six weeks of Q2. If advertising picks up because engagement is through the roof during the World Cup—"

**BEARXTER**: "That's a lot of 'ifs.'"

**BULLXTER**: "They're both real ifs. Not invented. Ad recovery is documented — three to four percent growth. World Cup engagement on Snap is a certainty — the only question is the dollar amount. The option is seventeen dollars. What's the worst case?"

**BEARXTER** *(grudgingly)*: "The worst case is we lose seventeen dollars."

**PRIME**: "The Quantxter concern: Snap is not near its lows. The fifty-two week range is three eighty-one to ten forty-one. The stock is at five seventy-two — that's fifty-four percent of the way from the low to the high. Mid-range. Not the beaten-down setup we prefer."

**BULLXTER**: "But the option pricing behaves like it's near the lows. Because it's a five-dollar stock, the options are optionally priced. We're getting low-stock-price option economics without requiring the stock to actually be at lows."

**QUANTXTER**: "The expected value on the seven-call at seventeen cents is positive. At the analyst consensus — seven sixty-three — the option is worth sixty-three cents. On seventeen cents, that's three-point-seven times. Probability of reaching consensus by July 17: approximately thirty-eight percent given the recovery trajectory. Probability of profit: roughly forty percent."

"However. The options at this price level are likely illiquid. The bid-ask spread on a seventeen-cent option may be five to ten cents wide. Real entry cost may be twenty-two to twenty-five cents. That changes the expected value marginally but not materially."

**BEARXTER**: "If I'm wrong about Snap being structurally impaired — if the advertising recovery is real and sustainable — this is the cheapest good trade on the board. Seventeen dollars to participate in a thirty-three percent upside story with a known catalyst date."

"I'm not saying it's a good company. I'm saying the option is priced for a bad company and the company might be less bad than the option assumes."

*He looked at the chain for a long moment.*

"Three out of five. Maybe three-point-five. Not four."

## THE THESIS

Q2 FY2026 (April-June) is the first CLEAN advertising quarter post-platform-rollback. World Cup runs June 11-July 19 — last 6 weeks of Q2 — driving massive Snapchat engagement and content creation. Younger demographics (Snap's core) are exactly the World Cup audience. If engagement drives ad spending increases, Q2 could show advertising recovery is real, not a one-quarter blip.

The structural concern (TikTok competition) is real but not new. The question is whether Snap has found a stable floor and the advertising recovery represents normalization, not a temporary bounce.

---

## THE RISK

Snap has disappointed multiple quarters in a row. The advertising platform was damaged by their own changes and the recovery may be slower than hoped. TikTok continues to take share in the same demographic. If Q2 shows revenue flat or down, the stock falls from $5.72 toward $4 and the option expires worthless. Seventeen dollars lost.

---

## VERDICT

**SNAP: 3/5 — genuine interest, not 4/5 conviction.**

The option price is the most compelling thing about this trade. $17 at risk is extraordinary — we can run 3-5 contracts and still be Iron Rule compliant. The July 16 earnings catalyst is real. The analyst consensus is above the break-even.

What's missing from the 4/5 bar: the stock isn't near its lows (mid-range), the consensus is "Neutral" not "Strong Buy," and Snap has a documented pattern of disappointing. Without insider buying and without being at multi-year lows, it doesn't match the ABT or original NKE structure.

But at $17 per contract, Bearxter is willing to watch it. The risk is contained.
