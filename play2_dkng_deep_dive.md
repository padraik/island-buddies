## BEARXTER'S QUESTION: WHY DID IT FALL FROM $53 TO $25?

**The answer: guidance cut, not business deterioration.**

In February 2026, DraftKings reported Q4 2025 earnings:
- Revenue: $1.989B (+43% YoY) — strong
- EPS: $0.36 vs $0.50 consensus — miss

More importantly, they issued FY2026 guidance:
- Revenue: **$6.7B** vs consensus of **$7.3B** (missed by $600M)
- Adj. EBITDA: **$800M** vs consensus of **$980M** (missed by $180M)

Stock fell 13.5% that day. The fall was guidance-driven from an already-declining position since the $53 high.

The business grew 43% in Q4 2025. The problem was Wall Street expected faster. This is sector multiple compression on a real business, not a broken model — the same structure as NKE's March 2024 decline. The guidance isn't "we're losing money indefinitely." It's "we're profitable, just not at your timeline."

## THE PREDICTION MARKETS PIVOT

At their 2026 Investor Day, Robins announced a strategic shift that wasn't on anyone's radar when the stock was at $53.

**What it is:** Prediction markets — think Kalshi and Polymarket, but inside the mainstream DraftKings app. Events you can bet on: will this team win, will this economic indicator hit X, will this movie gross $Y, will this political event happen. Regulated, mainstream, legal.

**The numbers:**
- Projected opportunity: **$10B annual gross revenue**
- Stated goal: market leader by end of 2026
- Vehicle: unified "DraftKings Sports & Casino" Super App — sportsbook, iGaming, prediction markets, and Jackpocket lottery under one wallet

**Why this matters structurally:**

Traditional sportsbook carries house risk — DraftKings makes money when bettors lose, loses money when bettors win. Prediction markets operate differently: the platform takes a fee on every transaction regardless of outcome. Lower variance, potentially higher margins.

More importantly: prediction markets expand the addressable market beyond sports fans. You don't have to care about the NFL to bet on whether inflation hits 3% next quarter. This is a product for the general internet population, not just sports bettors.

**The product is launching now.** This upside isn't priced into a stock sitting at $25.

## THE NORMIE ADOPTION THESIS

This isn't in the earnings model. This is the decade case.

The UK has had mainstream sports betting for thirty years. You walk into a British pub and there's a betting terminal. Penetration rates are dramatically higher than the US. America is earlier on the same adoption curve — the direction is not in question, only the timeline.

The NFL has already done the hard work. Broadcast integrations, point spread graphics on screen, ESPN anchors discussing lines — the cultural stigma around betting is functionally gone. The casual fan who would never have placed a bet in 2015 checks the spread in 2026.

Prediction markets accelerate this further. Sports betting still requires caring about sports. "Will Taylor Swift win Album of the Year?" doesn't. The Super App is designed to capture the next wave of users who never touched a sportsbook.

**The demographic tailwind:** Gen Z is more comfortable with gambling than any previous generation. As they move into peak earning years over the next decade, DraftKings is the brand already on their phones.

**The regulatory risk is real** but the direction of travel is clear. More states are legalizing, not fewer. The social harm backlash scenario exists but hasn't materialized despite years of opportunity.

At $25, you're not paying for any of this. You're buying a profitable, growing business at 22x forward earnings with a high-margin second engine launching this year and a decade of normalization tailwind behind it.
