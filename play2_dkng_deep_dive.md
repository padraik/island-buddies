## BEARXTER'S QUESTION: WHY DID IT FALL FROM $53 TO $25?

**The answer: guidance cut, not business deterioration.**

In February 2026, DraftKings reported Q4 2025 earnings:
- Revenue: $1.989B (+43% YoY) — strong
- EPS: $0.36 vs $0.50 consensus estimate — miss

More importantly, they issued FY2026 guidance:
- Revenue: **$6.7B** vs consensus of **$7.3B** (missed by $600M)
- Adj. EBITDA: **$800M** vs consensus of **$980M** (missed by $180M)

Stock fell 13.5% that day. From an already-declining position since the $53 high.

**What this means for the thesis:**

The business grew 43% in Q4 2025. The problem was Wall Street expected faster. The market re-priced because profitability is arriving slower than modeled — but it IS arriving. The guidance isn't "we're losing money indefinitely." It's "we're profitable, just not at your timeline."

This is sector multiple compression on a real business, not a broken model. The comparison: NKE fell because China and inventory problems were bigger than expected. The business wasn't dying — it was recovering more slowly. That's exactly what happened to DKNG.

## UNIT ECONOMICS: IMPROVING

This is where the bull case gets structural.

**Hold rate trend:**
| Quarter | Hold Rate |
|---|---|
| Q1 2025 | 6.4% |
| Q1 2026 | **7.8%** (+1.4 ppts) |

Hold rate = how much of all wagered money becomes revenue. 1.4 percentage points on $6B+ in handle = ~$84M in additional annual revenue from the same volume of bets. This is margin expansion without requiring more customers.

**Promotional spending:**
Early-stage sports betting markets required 25–30% of handle in promotions to acquire customers. Mature markets are now at 10–15%. DraftKings' market is maturing — the promo spend required to keep and grow customers is declining.

**Market structure:**
- DraftKings (34%) + FanDuel (44%) = **78% of US sports betting revenue**
- Combined duopoly, like Visa/Mastercard in payments
- Smaller competitors (ESPN Bet, BetMGM, Fanatics) are splitting the remaining 22%
- Scale advantages compound: technology infrastructure, regulatory compliance, data costs — all spread over a larger revenue base

**Customer acquisition:**
- Industry CAC: $300–500 per first-time depositor
- DraftKings reducing CAC through prediction market integration
- World Cup is a **rare mass customer acquisition event** — millions of casual bettors create accounts, many stay for NFL season

The economics are getting better, not worse. The guidance cut was about timeline, not trajectory.
