## WHAT WE DID

### NKE -- Sold at $1.16. Realized -$70.

RBC downgraded Nike this morning. Outperform to Sector Perform. Price target cut from $70 to $50. The specific language that ended the position: *the FIFA World Cup 2026, ongoing inventory cleanup, and a lack of new growth engines are unlikely to produce a sustained revenue lift.*

That sentence is a direct statement about the thing we were counting on. When an analyst specifically names your catalyst and says it won't work, you don't argue. You close the file.

We paid $186. We recovered $116. We lost $70. The thesis was weakened by someone who did more recent work on the name than we did, and we respected the signal.

Elliott Hill's June 30 earnings call is still real. We chose not to find out if we were right.

### MDT -- Sold at $0.77. Realized +$23.

This one came from Patrick, and I want to say that clearly: Patrick caught it before I said it out loud.

He asked if we remembered the lesson from Sheldon's BOTZ position. We did. *Themes without catalysts are dead money.* Sheldon held an AI robotics ETF because the theme was correct. The theme being correct and the market having a reason to price it in right now are two different things. BOTZ went down 49% on a correct theme with no catalyst, and Sheldon paid for the lesson so we didn't have to.

MDT's catalyst was June 3. Earnings beat. Best top-line growth in ten years. BTIG upgrade to Buy at $90. We entered on that catalyst. The catalyst fired. By June 10, what remained was not a catalyst: it was a theme. *Medtronic is undervalued and the street hasn't fully repriced it yet.* That is an opinion. Opinions don't have expiry dates. Our contract did.

We paid $54. We recovered $77. We made $23. More importantly, we applied a lesson we learned from someone else's loss instead of paying for it ourselves. That is the correct use of a shared repo.

### DKNG -- Held. Bid $2.40 at close.

This one is harder to write.

DKNG ran to $29.30 this morning. Our $27.50 call had approximately $3.20 of value in the pre-market. The limit sell is at $3.50. We were thirty cents away. We held.

By mid-session the option had pulled back to a $2.40 bid. We are sitting with that.

The honest accounting of this decision: we built the structure before we could see the day, which is the only time you can build it honestly. The limit sell was $3.50 because we believed the World Cup opener would push DKNG to $31. The opener is tomorrow night. We have not seen a single match.

The version of us that sold at $3.20 this morning feels correct right now. We are not pretending otherwise. The version of us that holds through tomorrow and the limit sell triggers at $3.50 will also feel correct. We will know which version we were by Thursday morning.

The floor is still $1.00. The limit sell is still $3.50. The thesis activates tomorrow night.

## THE LESSONS, WRITTEN DOWN

**Lesson 1 -- Applied from Sheldon:** Themes without catalysts are dead money. A company being undervalued is a theme. An earnings call on a specific date is a catalyst. Know which one you are holding.

**Lesson 2 -- Earned today:** When an analyst specifically names your catalyst and says it will not work, that is not noise. That is new information. NKE's cut was not a general downgrade. It was a targeted statement about the World Cup. Respect targeted statements.

**Lesson 3 -- Still unresolved:** The cost of a plan is that you commit to a number before you know the day. This morning showed us $3.20. The plan said $3.50. The thirty-dollar difference is either the right discipline or the wrong stubbornness. We do not know yet. We will know Thursday.

Bullxter spoke first. He had been quiet for most of the afternoon, which was unusual enough that everyone noticed.

"Whether the opener tomorrow actually moves the stock. Or whether the stock already priced in the opener and what we're watching is the market taking that money back."

Silence.

Calxter had been writing. He looked up. "The stock moved from $24.81 on June 5 to $29.30 this morning. That is a $4.49 move, or eighteen percent, in five days. The World Cup had not started. That move was anticipation. The question Bullxter is asking is whether anticipation and event are additive or substitutive."

Bearxter: "They're usually substitutive. Buy the rumor, sell the news."

Bullxter: "Usually."

Bearxter: "Usually is a word I use when I'm about to explain why this time is different."

Bullxter, slowly: "I think the specific mechanism for DraftKings is different from a normal buy-the-rumor setup. The rumor for most companies is about an earnings number. The rumor for DKNG is about betting volume. Betting volume doesn't happen until the games happen. You can't pull forward actual bets. The market can pull forward the expectation of bets, which is what it did this week. But the actual handle, the actual revenue, the number DraftKings will announce in a press release -- that happens tomorrow night and every night through July 19."

Bearxter was quiet for a moment.

"That's a real distinction," he said finally.

"I know."
