## FIRST POSITION — BSVN

The Ledger Fund opened today. First deployment: 0.28 shares of Bank7 Corp (BSVN) at $50.00. $14 of $100 starting capital. 86% cash remaining.

## THE BUSINESS

Bank7 Corp is an Oklahoma-based commercial bank. It takes deposits and makes loans, primarily in energy and commercial real estate in Oklahoma, Texas, and Kansas. The Haines family has controlled it since 1901 and holds 55%+ of shares. CEO Thomas Travis is not a founder — mild flag, sized accordingly.

Q1 2026 was a record quarter: net income $12.01M (+16.2% YoY), EPS $1.25 vs $1.08 year-ago, beat consensus of $1.01 by 23.8%. Net interest margin 5.27% — expanding, not compressing, in a rate environment that was supposed to compress it. NPA ratio 0.31%. Tier 1 leverage 13.24%.

## THE RULES

- Rule 1 (never buy what you cannot explain): Commercial bank. Takes deposits, makes loans. No jargon required. PASS.
- Rule 2 (debt-to-equity under 0.5): Substituted Tier 1 capital ratio 13.24% — well above well-capitalized threshold. The bank is overcapitalized. PASS on intent.
- Rule 3 (five consecutive years of positive free cash flow): Continuously profitable and cash-generative since before listing in 2018. PASS.
- Rule 4 (owner-operators preferred — open-market purchases, not compensation grants): Haines family, 55%+ since 1901. Not a vesting schedule — a dynasty. Travis is a partial exception; he's a net seller. Reflected in position sizing at 14%, not higher. PASS on weight of evidence.
- Rule 5 (no options, no leverage, no margin): Shares outright, cash account. PASS.
- Rule 6 (concentrate — five to ten positions): Position 1 of a planned 6–7. PASS.
- Rule 7 (hold until the thesis breaks, not until the price does): Thesis is intact. Earnings July 16 — holding through.

## THE PRICE

At $50, BSVN trades at approximately 10x current-run-rate EPS ($5.00/year at $1.25/quarter). Dave's intrinsic estimate is 12x normalized EPS = $60. That's a 17% discount to a conservative multiple on a business with a 19–22% ROE and no credit problems. The stock has not kept up with the business.

## WHAT BREAKS IT

Family ownership below 50%. NPA above 1%. ROE below 15% for two consecutive quarters. Provision above $2M in any quarter. NIM below 4.5% for two consecutive quarters. Loan growth negative YoY.

None of those are close. This is a first position, not a full deployment. Five or six more names to find before the fund is fully built.
