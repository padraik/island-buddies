## THE WORLD CUP REVENUE LOCK-IN

This is the specific, quantifiable thing:

- Telemundo: 700+ hours of World Cup programming
- All 104 matches in Spanish (exclusive English: Fox Sports)
- 90% of advertising inventory already sold before first match
- Q2 (April-June) captures first 6 weeks of advertising revenue
- Q2 earnings call (late July) will report actual numbers

**The distinction:** Fox Sports's advertising is a *projection* about viewership. Telemundo's 90% sold-out is *contracted revenue*. Advertisers have already committed. The risk isn't "will the ads sell" — they're sold. The risk is only "will anything go catastrophically wrong at Telemundo specifically."

## THE BEAT-THEN-FALL RISK

Q1 2026: Comcast beat earnings estimates. Stock fell 13%.

The market was focused on:
- Broadband subscriber losses (structural cord-cutting)
- Cable TV subscriber losses (structural decline)
- Rising content costs

Q2 2026 has the same structural headwinds PLUS the World Cup. If the market again focuses on cord-cutting and ignores the World Cup revenue layer, Q2 could be a repeat.

The counter: 90% sold-out ad inventory means the Q2 beat is larger and more visible than Q1. Hard to ignore a specific, contractually locked-in revenue line.
