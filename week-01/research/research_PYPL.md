# PAYPAL HOLDINGS (PYPL) — RESEARCH FILE
*June 1, 1996. The timing problem.*

---

## THE BAXTER CONVERSATION

**BAXTER-PRIME**: "PayPal. Online payments. Venmo. They've been losing ground to Apple Pay, Google Pay, smaller competitors. Stock is at forty-four ninety — fifty-two-week range is thirty-eight to seventy-nine. Near the low end. Beat Q1 EPS by five-and-a-half percent. Earnings July 28th."

**BEARXTER**: "What's the structural problem."

**PRIME**: "Strongest competitive pressure in nearly thirty years, per the analyst commentary. Their core checkout business is declining. The margins they had when they were the only online payments game are compressing."

**BEARXTER**: "So we're buying a structurally impaired business near its lows."

**BULLXTER**: "We're buying a structurally impaired business that still has four hundred million active accounts, that just beat earnings, that has Venmo growing, that has a twenty-one dollar spread between where it is and where analysts think it should be."

**PRIME**: "Here's the instrument problem." He opens the chain. "Earnings are July 28th. The options jump from July 17th directly to September 18th. There's nothing in between."

*Silence.*

**BULLXTER**: "Can we use July 17?"

**PRIME**: "July 17 expires eleven days before the earnings catalyst. We'd be betting on the stock drifting from forty-four to fifty before the actual data point. With no specific reason for it to drift."

**BEARXTER**: "And September 18?"

**PRIME**: "The $52.50 call at September 18 costs seventy-nine cents, break-even fifty-eight twenty-nine. Analyst consensus is fifty-two fifty-three. We need the stock to go above analyst consensus just to break even."

**BEARXTER**: "Both options are structurally wrong."

**BULLXTER**: "What about July 17 as a pure momentum play? If the market re-rates PayPal on any positive news between now and July 17—"

**BEARXTER**: "We don't play 'if the market feels like it.' We play defined catalysts."

*Prime closes the binder.*

**PRIME**: "If PayPal had an August expiration, this would be a real conversation. The July 17 options are affordable — fifty dollar call at sixty-two cents, break-even fifty-point-sixty-two, consensus well above that. The setup is fine. The timing isn't. Earnings land in the gap between our best option and our next one."

**BULLXTER**: "So the trade is real, just the instrument is broken."

**BEARXTER**: "The instrument and the structural thesis. Both are real problems."

## THE CHAIN (verified live, June 1, 2026)

**The timing problem:** Options jump from July 17 to September 18. No expiration captures July 28 earnings.

**July 17 (45d, expires 11 days BEFORE earnings):**
- $50C at $0.62 = $62 at risk, break-even $50.62 — consensus ($52.53) is above break-even ✅
- But option expires before the earnings catalyst ❌

**September 18 (108d, captures earnings but...):**
- $52.50C at $0.79 = $79 at risk, break-even $53.97 (+20.2%)
- Analyst consensus $52.53 is BELOW the break-even ❌

Neither option captures the catalyst correctly.

## VERDICT

**PYPL: PASS — timing structure fails on both available options.**

PayPal is genuinely near its 52-week lows. The Q1 beat is real. The stock has clear analyst upside to $52.53. But there is no options expiration that captures the July 28 earnings catalyst within Iron Rule constraints AND has a break-even below analyst consensus.

If there were a July 31 expiration, the $50C would be a compelling 3/5 trade. That expiration doesn't exist.

Structural note: PayPal's competitive position is genuinely challenged. This isn't the same "bad news already priced in, good business" story as ABT. The pressure is ongoing and the recovery thesis is less clear.

*"The trade is the window," Bearxter said. "And the window isn't there."*
