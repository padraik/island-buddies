**BULLXTER**: "I don't have the exact breakdown. Stock-based comp, maybe. Or one-time items."

**PRIME**: "That's the question. Lyft competes with Uber on price in a market where the product is nearly identical. The way you win is driver availability and surge pricing. **BEARXTER**: "What makes August 12 different?"

**BULLXTER**: "Q2 captures summer travel. More rides, more demand. If the operating leverage shows up in EPS the way EBITDA suggests it should — if Q1's EPS miss was a timing issue — Q2 cleans it up."

**BEARXTER**: "That's an if."

**BULLXTER**: "Everything is an if. The option is eighty-six dollars. Let Quantxter run the numbers."

**QUANTXTER** *(looking up from his pad)*: "At analyst consensus of nineteen oh-two, the August 21 seventeen-dollar call is worth two dollars and two cents. "EV at thirty-eight percent: positive but modest. Expected value approximately sixty-two percent on the eighty-six cent option — below ABT's model."

**BEARXTER**: "So we make money on average, but ABT is better."

**QUANTXTER**: "ABT has stronger insider signal, more analyst support relative to price, and a more specific catalyst. Lyft has more optionality at the upper end — if Lyft recovers to its fifty-two week high of twenty-five fifty-four, the seventeen-dollar call is worth eight fifty-four. Nine hundred percent. But the probability of that is low."

**PRIME**: "Here's the honest comparison. ABT: CEO bought two million dollars of his own money at a hundred-and-seven, which is twenty-five percent above current. Every analyst on the street thinks it's worth more than our break-even. First full quarter of a forty-billion-dollar oncology acquisition. That's a four-out-of-five."

"Lyft: near lows, thirteen Buy analysts, EBITDA growing, Q2 could clean up the EPS miss. But no insider buying. One analyst with a Sell. The 'why is this quarter different' answer is 'maybe the Q1 EPS miss was one-time.' Maybe."

"Three out of five."

**BULLXTER**: "Still in range."

**PRIME**: "Still in range. Watch it. If Q1's EPS miss gets explained and Q2 guidance is solid, this moves toward three-and-a-half."

## THE THESIS

Lyft near 52-week lows after multi-year stabilization. EBITDA growing 25% YoY shows the unit economics are improving even if Q1 EPS disappointed. Summer Q2 (May-July) captures peak ride-share demand. August 12 earnings should show whether Q1's EPS miss was structural or timing.

The autonomous vehicle threat (Waymo, Tesla Robotaxi) has been priced into the stock for two years but hasn't materially disrupted the core business. At $14 with analyst consensus at $19, the market is pricing in continued decline. If Q2 shows stable or improving EPS alongside the strong bookings, the stock re-rates.

---

## THE RISK

The EPS miss of -42% in Q1 is a real concern. If profitability is structurally impaired — if SBC is systematically overstated, or if driver economics are worsening — the business story doesn't match the EBITDA story. Q2 could disappoint again. The stock falls from $14 toward $12 and we lose $86.

Also: no insider buying. No CEO putting $2M on the line. The conviction signal that made ABT compelling isn't here.

---

## VERDICT

**LYFT: 3/5 — genuine interest, below the 4/5 bar.**

The chain works. The timing works. The analyst support is real. Options are affordable and Iron Rule compliant.

What's missing: the "specific catalyst that makes this quarter different" answer is "maybe Q1's EPS miss was one-time." That's not as clean as ABT's "first full quarter of a $21B acquisition and CEO is buying with his own money."

Watch it. If Q1's EPS shortfall gets explained in the next data point and summer metrics look strong, revisit at 3.5/5.

The $17C at $0.86 is a real option on a real company near its lows with real analyst support. It's just not quite four.
