# VARONIS SYSTEMS (VRNS) + FTI CONSULTING (FCN) — COMBINED FILE
*June 1, 1996. Two insider buying signals. Two chain failures.*

---

## VARONIS SYSTEMS (VRNS)

### The Baxter Conversation

**BAXTER-PRIME**: "Varonis. Data security software. CEO bought twenty-six thousand shares with his own money in February. CFO bought. CTO bought. Combined they put in over a million dollars. The stock is at thirty-four seventy-five — fifty-two-week range is nineteen seventy to sixty-three ninety. Near the lower third. Analyst consensus: forty-six seventy-one. Seventeen Buy ratings, zero Sell."

**BULLXTER**: "That's the ABT signal. Multiple insiders, cluster buy, open market, their own money. At an independent company that kind of coordinated buy usually means they see something."

**BEARXTER**: "When did they buy?"

**PRIME**: "February. Average price around twenty-two dollars."

**BEARXTER**: "Stock is now at thirty-four seventy-five. They're already up fifty-eight percent from their buy price."

**BULLXTER**: "Still forty-six percent below analyst consensus."

**PRIME**: "Let me check the chain." *Pause.* "September 18th, which captures the July 28th earnings. Cheapest option: fifty-dollar call at a dollar fifty. That's a hundred and fifty dollars at risk."

**BEARXTER**: "Seventy-five percent of the fund. Iron Rule violation."

**PRIME**: "The forty-dollar call is three-thirty. The forty-five call is two-twenty-five. Nothing under a hundred at risk in any expiration."

**BULLXTER**: "High IV. Software company, beaten down, the market is pricing significant uncertainty into the options."

**BEARXTER**: "Which means the options are expensive exactly when the stock looks cheapest. Same problem we've seen before."

**PRIME**: "The insider signal is real. The thesis is real. The instrument doesn't fit our fund. Pass."

**Verdict: PASS — insider buying signal is real, options too expensive for Iron Rule.**

## FTI CONSULTING (FCN)

### The Baxter Conversation

**BAXTER-PRIME**: "FTI Consulting. Management consulting — restructuring, litigation support, forensic accounting. Three senior executives bought fourteen thousand four hundred shares on the same day at a hundred and forty-four. Combined: two million dollars. CEO, CFO, and Chief Strategy Officer, all buying in the open market at the same time. Classic cluster signal."

**BULLXTER**: "That's as clean as it gets. Three C-suite executives all deciding simultaneously to put millions in."

**BEARXTER**: "What's the stock at now?"

**PRIME**: "A hundred and fifty-three."

**BEARXTER**: "They bought at a hundred and forty-four. It's already up six percent since they bought."

**PRIME**: "Analyst target is a hundred and seventy-four. Upside from here is still fourteen percent."

**BEARXTER**: "Only two analysts cover the company. Hold rating. And — what does the chain look like?"

**PRIME**: "June 18: hundred-seventy call at a dollar sixty-five — a hundred and sixty-five at risk. July 17: no options in the zero-to-two dollar range at all. September 18: two-hundred call at a dollar eighty-five."

**BEARXTER**: "So there's no option at any expiration that captures the earnings and fits the Iron Rule."

**PRIME**: "Correct. This is a stock where the insider signal is strong but the chain has nothing for us. A hundred-and-fifty-three dollar stock with low liquidity options."

**BULLXTER**: "The signal was right — they bought at a hundred and forty-four and it's already at a hundred and fifty-three. We just don't have a vehicle."

**PRIME**: "We file it and wait for a pullback to where the signal was. If it comes back to a hundred and forty, the thesis hasn't changed and the signal still stands."

| Analyst consensus | $174.50 |
| Cheapest viable option | $170C Jun18 at $1.65 = **$165** (OVER) |

**Verdict: PASS — strong insider signal, options too expensive, stock has already moved off buy price.**

---

## THE LESSON

Both names had the insider buying signal that made ABT a 4/5. Both had options that violated the Iron Rule for different reasons:
- VRNS: high implied volatility on beaten-down software makes every option expensive
- FCN: low options liquidity on a thinly-covered stock, no viable strike in range

The insider buying screen works. The instrument screen is the second filter. Both are necessary.
