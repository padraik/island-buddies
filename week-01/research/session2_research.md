## DEEP DIVE — NKE (Nike)

### The Real Thesis (Not the Naive One)

Session 1 called NKE a "turnaround story." That was incomplete. Here's the honest picture:

**Q4 FY2026 (June 25) will be deliberately bad:**
- Management guided revenue DOWN 2-4%
- Greater China guided DOWN ~20%
- EPS consensus: $0.12 (vs. $1.01 same quarter FY2025 — down 88%)
- Management explicitly called Q4 the **"low point"** of their Win Now transformation
- Nike Direct still declining. Converse collapsed 30% in Q2. China recovery "through FY2027."

**This sounds terrible. Here's why it might still work:**

**The "Low Point" Setup:** When management pre-announces a bad quarter AND calls it the bottom, the market has usually already priced in the pain. The stock at $46 is already reflecting a struggling company. The question at earnings isn't "how bad is Q4" — the market knows it's bad. The question is: **"Is FY2027 actually going to be better?"**

**The Bull Scenario (why options could 3-5x):**
1. Q4 results come in at or slightly above the lowered bar (even $0.13 EPS beats $0.12)
2. Management gives FY2027 guidance that shows gross margin expansion beginning Q2 FY27 (already telegraphed as possible)
3. North America shows continued strength (was +9% in Q2) — proving the core business works
4. Any positive data point on China stabilization (even "declining less fast")
5. Stock pops 8-12% on relief + FY2027 optimism

**The Bear Scenario (why we could lose the premium):**
1. Q4 comes in at the low end of guidance or worse
2. FY2027 guidance disappoints — management pushes recovery to FY2028
3. China keeps declining at 20%+
4. Tariff impact worse than expected ($1B headwind for new fiscal year)
5. Stock drops 8-12%

**Historical earnings reactions:** After Q3 FY2026 (April 2 earnings): NKE dropped 0.4% immediately, then drifted +3% over 56 days. NKE does NOT historically make huge moves on earnings even in bad quarters. This is both reassuring (limited downside) and concerning (limited upside even on a beat).

**The Core Risk/Reward Question:** If the stock typically moves 3-8% on earnings and our call costs $1.50, we need at least a 7-8% move for 3x. That's achievable on a beat + good guidance. But it's not a layup.

**Conviction: 3.5/5** — Upgraded from the session 1 narrative but with more realistic expectations. This is a controlled risk bet on "worst already priced in + FY2027 optimism."
