### The Setup
This is the rare setup where the DOWNSIDE is defined by a third party's wallet. Beretta wants more shares at $44.80. Stock at ~$38.46 = ~16% upside to the tender price with a soft floor underneath. Zero debt, ~$92-105M cash, 75-year clean balance sheet. This is the closest thing to a "bond with upside" in our watchlist.

### What Could Go Wrong
The tender "has not commenced and requires regulatory approvals" — that's the catch. If regulators block or delay it, the floor is a PROMISE, not a guarantee, and the stock falls back to ~$34 on fundamentals (which are deteriorating — that EPS miss matters). Options liquidity on a $613M small-cap is typically thin/wide — I'd need to verify the chain before ANY options play; this may be a shares-only or covered name. Earnings are weak, so the only thing holding it up is the deal.

## GLOSSARY
- **Tender offer:** A public bid to buy shares at a set price ($44.80); a partial tender buys up to a cap, putting a soft floor under the stock.
- **Standstill:** A contractual limit on how large a stake the acquirer (Beretta) can take and for how long.
- **Debit/shares play:** With thin options, owning the stock directly avoids wide bid/ask spreads.
