### The Setup
This is the most "me" thing on the board: zero debt for decades, $105M cash, 3.5:1 current ratio, and an acquirer who already owns ~10% and wants ~30%. Downside is cushioned by cash on the balance sheet and a buyer in the market. ### What Could Go Wrong
1. The tender could FAIL or get pulled, removing the floor — stock drifts back toward $34.
2. Firearms demand is cyclical and politically sensitive; a soft consumer in a slowing economy hurts unit volumes.
3. Options liquidity on a small-cap like RGR is THIN — wide bid/ask, hard to exit. For us, that argues SHARES, not options.
4. Board fight could turn ugly and drag on with no resolution = dead money.
