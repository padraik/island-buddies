# THE FIVE BAXTERS -- STZ OR CCL (OR BOTH?)
*June 1. Late. Baxter's room. The binder is open to the week-two section.*
*Patrick just hung up. All five are present. This one needs everyone.*

---

## OPENING -- BAXTER-PRIME

**BAXTER-PRIME** set the two chains side by side on the desk.

"Two plays. One fund. We need to figure out if we're entering one, both, or neither. Calxter goes first."

---

## CALXTER SETS THE TABLE

**CALXTER** had already done the math.

"Fund state as of tonight. Total capital: five hundred dollars. Deployed: three hundred sixty-four. NKE one hundred eighty-six, ABT seventy-eight, HITI one hundred -- limit order placed tonight, fills at open tomorrow. Reserve remaining: one hundred thirty-six dollars in SGOV."

He looked up.

"That number matters because it constrains everything we're about to discuss. At one hundred thirty-six dollars in available capital, we cannot enter both plays at the sizes we analyzed earlier today. The STZ one-fifty-five call at one-eighty is fifteen dollars over reserve. The CCL thirty call at one-forty-eight is twelve dollars over reserve. Both together are one hundred ninety-six dollars over reserve. We are choosing one, or we are choosing neither."

Silence.

"There are affordable alternatives. STZ has a one-sixty call on July 10 at one twenty-five. CCL has a thirty-one call on July 17 at one-oh-seven. Both fit within one thirty-six. Combined they cost two thirty-two, which does not fit. One or the other."

**PRIME**: "Walk through both."

---

## STZ -- CONSTELLATION BRANDS

**BULLXTER**: "Modelo Especial. The number one selling beer in the United States. The World Cup starts in ten days. One hundred and four matches on American soil. Management called it an outstanding opportunity on their last call. The June thirtieth earnings is the first call with nineteen days of live tournament data in hand. The CEO will have real World Cup sales numbers sitting in front of him. Not projections. Sales."

**BEARXTER**: "Walk me through the chain."

**PRIME**: "One-sixty call on July tenth. One twenty-five at risk. Break-even one sixty-one twenty-five. Current price one thirty-six ninety-four."

*He paused.*

"BofA has an Underperform at one fifty-two. That is nine dollars and twenty-five cents below our break-even."

**BEARXTER**: "That is a larger gap than I want."

**BULLXTER**: "BofA is one analyst out of twenty-three. Their target is eleven percent above where the stock trades today. They are not saying the stock falls. They are saying the upside is limited. Twenty-two of twenty-three analysts have targets above our break-even. The consensus is one seventy-seven."

**BEARXTER**: "For NKE, Goldman's target was fourteen cents above our break-even. For ABT, Leerink was five dollars above our break-even. For STZ on the one-sixty call, the most bearish major analyst is nine dollars and twenty-five cents below. That is not the same standard."

**PRIME**: "Acknowledged. What would change that math?"

**CALXTER**: "The original analysis used the one-fifty-five call at one-eighty. Break-even one fifty-six thirty-five. BofA's one-fifty-two is only four thirty-five below that break-even. The tighter option was better. The fund constraint is what pushed us to the one-sixty."

**BEARXTER**: "So the fund constraint made a marginal play worse."

**CALXTER**: "Correct. At one-fifty-five, the gap to BofA was four thirty-five. At one-sixty, the gap is nine twenty-five. The thesis didn't change. The instrument changed because of capital."

*Macxter, who had been quiet, spoke.*

**MACXTER**: "One thing on STZ that has not been said. BofA's Underperform. That rating has been on this stock since before the World Cup was announced. Their analyst, Peter Galbo, cut the target from one fifty-four to one fifty-two on updated models. He did not change his thesis on the World Cup. His concern is tariff exposure on Mexican imports -- Modelo is brewed in Mexico -- and volume trajectory in the beer segment. Neither of those concerns has a catalyst on June thirtieth. What June thirtieth does have is the first quarter with World Cup data. BofA's bear case is real but it is a twelve-month view. Not a thirty-nine-day view."

**BEARXTER**: "That is worth noting. His twelve-month target and a thirty-nine-day option are different questions."

**PRIME**: "So the argument is: even if BofA is right in twelve months, the stock can trade above our break-even for six weeks on earnings momentum."

**BULLXTER**: "The stock was at one fifty-three in May. It was there two weeks ago. We are not asking it to do something it hasn't already done this year."

**CALXTER**: "At analyst consensus of one seventy-seven, the one-sixty call on July ten is worth seventeen dollars. Return on one twenty-five: plus one thousand two hundred sixty percent. At BofA's bear target of one fifty-two, the option expires worthless. We lose one twenty-five. The probability of being above the break-even of one sixty-one at July tenth expiry: I estimate thirty-eight to forty-two percent given the earnings catalyst, current price position, and the beta of zero-point-four-two."

**BEARXTER**: "Expected value?"

**CALXTER**: "At forty percent probability of winning an average of six dollars per share: positive. At thirty-five percent, still positive. The math works. The comfort level is what's in question, not the math."

---

## CCL -- CARNIVAL CORPORATION

**BULLXTER**: "Net profit up fifty-point-nine percent year over year. Twenty-one of twenty-six analysts bullish. Zero Sell ratings. The biggest cruise line in the world at forty-five percent of its fifty-two week range. Earnings in twenty-two days."

**BEARXTER**: "The minimum analyst target."

**PRIME**: "Twenty-eight seventy. The instrument we're discussing is the thirty-one call at one-oh-seven. Break-even thirty-two oh-seven."

**BEARXTER**: "Bear target is three thirty-seven below break-even. Worse gap than STZ on a percentage basis."

**BULLXTER**: "The twenty-eight seventy floor is Truist at a Hold. The most recent action today was Loop Capital initiating at thirty-six dollars. The analyst trend on CCL is moving up, not down. Loop's thirty-six target is three dollars and ninety-three cents above our break-even. The direction of coverage matters."

**BEARXTER**: "Morgan Stanley cut to twenty-five in May."

**BULLXTER**: "Loop initiated at thirty-six today. One is a month old. One is today."

**BEARXTER**: "Both are on the record."

**CALXTER**: "The minimum target from the summary table is twenty-eight seventy. That is current, which means it incorporates the Loop initiation and presumably retired the Morgan Stanley twenty-five. The floor moved up. Twenty-eight seventy is still below our break-even of thirty-two. The gap exists regardless of which data point we use."

**PRIME**: "What is the upside scenario?"

**CALXTER**: "At analyst consensus of thirty-four ten, the thirty-one call is worth three dollars and ten cents. Return on one-oh-seven: plus one hundred ninety percent. At Loop's thirty-six target, the option is worth five dollars. Return: plus three hundred sixty-eight percent."

**BULLXTER**: "The cruise sector has zero negative analyst coverage. That does not happen often. Someone who knows the business well enough to cover it looked at it and said the worst outcome is twenty-eight seventy -- not a crash, not bankruptcy, not a business problem. A measured conservative view that is three dollars below our break-even."

**BEARXTER**: "The sector has been in this condition for two years and the stock has not gone above thirty-four. The record high is thirty-four oh-three. At break-even thirty-two oh-seven, we need the stock to go to within two dollars of its all-time high. That requires a perfect earnings report."

**BULLXTER**: "Profit up fifty percent is not a perfect report. It's the minimum expectation."

**BEARXTER**: "Fair. The bear case here is not that the business disappoints. The bear case is that the stock has already priced in the good news and the earnings reaction is muted."

**PRIME**: "That is the beat-then-fall risk. Carnival beats, stock shrugs, we lose one-oh-seven."

**CALXTER**: "Probability of profit on the thirty-one call: approximately thirty-two to thirty-six percent, lower than STZ because we need a larger percentage move and the beta is higher, which makes the option more expensive relative to the underlying."

---

## THE DIRECT COMPARISON

**PRIME**: "Both plays have the same structural problem. The most pessimistic credible analyst has a target below our break-even. For STZ the gap is nine twenty-five. For CCL the gap is three thirty-seven. On that dimension, CCL is cleaner."

**BEARXTER**: "Agreed."

**PRIME**: "STZ has the World Cup thesis, which is specific, dated, and management-confirmed. CCL's catalyst is Q2 earnings with strong profit growth. Both are real."

**BULLXTER**: "STZ beats CCL on thesis quality. CCL beats STZ on the bear gap. STZ beats CCL on cost. CCL beats STZ on earnings proximity -- twenty-two days versus twenty-nine."

**CALXTER**: "On EV, STZ is marginally higher because the beta is lower, which means the option is cheaper relative to the expected move. A lower-beta stock with a specific catalyst at forty percent probability is better than a higher-beta stock at thirty-four percent probability."

**BEARXTER**: "I want to name what we're actually deciding. We ran the research hose all day. The market is near all-time highs. The clean four-out-of-five setups -- ABT and NKE -- existed because those stocks were at multi-year lows, not just fifty-two week lows, and even the most bearish analyst thought they were worth more than our break-even. We have not found that today. What we have found are two three-point-five-out-of-five setups. The question is whether three-point-five is enough to deploy capital."

Silence.

**PRIME**: "Is it?"

**BEARXTER**: "That is Patrick's call. Not mine."

**BULLXTER**: "Both have positive expected value. Both have defined catalysts. Both are Iron Rule compliant at the affordable strike. The only question is whether we hold out for four-out-of-five or act on the best available three-point-five."

**CALXTER**: "One additional consideration. We have three active positions as of tomorrow morning -- NKE, ABT, and HITI. Adding one more brings us to four concurrent positions with four separate catalyst dates: June fifteenth HITI, June twenty-three CCL if entered, June twenty-nine NKE, and June thirty STZ. All resolve before July seventeenth. That is a manageable calendar."

**MACXTER**: "NKE and STZ both report June thirtieth. That is two catalyst events on the same night. Worth noting, not disqualifying."

**PRIME**: "So the choice is STZ, CCL, or nothing."

*He looked at both chains.*

"Both have positive expected value. Both have specific catalyst dates in the next three to four weeks. Both have strong analyst support even if the bears are slightly below our break-even. The fund has one hundred thirty-six dollars remaining after HITI fills. We can afford one."

---

## THE VERDICT

**PRIME**: "STZ."

**BEARXTER**: "Why."

"Three reasons. One: the World Cup thesis is the most specific catalyst we've seen since ABT's Exact Sciences acquisition. Modelo is the number one beer in the country and the World Cup is happening in their home market for the first time in a generation. That is not a guess. That is a product in the right place at the right time with a specific date attached. Two: the BofA gap of nine twenty-five is on the one-sixty call specifically because of the fund constraint. The underlying thesis at the one-fifty-five call had a four-thirty-five gap -- smaller than our comfortable threshold but manageable. We're paying a gap-expansion tax on an instrument we wouldn't have needed if the reserve were larger. Three: June thirtieth is nineteen days after the World Cup starts. Elliott Hill at Nike will have nineteen days of live sales data. Management will have nineteen days of Modelo sales data. The call is not about the past quarter. It is about what is happening right now."

"CCL is a real play and I don't hate it. Profit up fifty percent and zero Sell ratings is not nothing. But the World Cup narrative connects more specifically to STZ than to cruises, and the BofA gap being above BofA's twelve-month target -- as opposed to BofA's thirty-nine-day view -- is a distinction Macxter correctly identified."

**BULLXTER**: "I wanted both."

**PRIME**: "I know."

**BEARXTER**: "STZ. On the record."

**CALXTER**: "Positive expected value. Confirmed."

**MACXTER**: "No Truth Social flags on STZ. No executive filing alerts. No regulatory concerns. Clean."
