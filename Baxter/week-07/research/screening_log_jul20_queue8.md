## GLOSSARY

- **Range percentile:** where the current price sits inside the 52-week low-to-high range, 0th = at the low. Calls funnel wants the bottom 20-25%.
- **MID-OUT:** stock in the 25th-74th percentile of range; neither calls nor puts candidate, killed at stage 1.
- **CALLS zone:** bottom quartile of 52-week range, eligible for the calls funnel.
- **Rule 2 (catalyst):** confirmed earnings date before the option's expiry. September reporters cannot pass with August expiries.
- **Rule 3 (ratings):** max 1 Sell/Underperform rating for calls. Hardened Jul 10: also requires ratings momentum not falling in the prior 30 days.
- **Rule 6 (reachability):** the required move to breakeven must be plausible against the stock's own historical earnings-day moves (cap ~1.5x the median reaction).
- **Breakeven:** strike + premium paid; the stock price where the call is worth what was paid at expiry.
- **STRETCH:** chain-script label for an ask at the top of the affordable band ($0.90-$1.00), sizing caution.
- **8-K:** an SEC filing companies use to disclose material events between quarters, here Shake Shack's own guidance cut.
- **Capitulation in progress:** analysts actively cutting targets/ratings inside the last 30 days; the funnel requires capitulation to be finished before entry, not underway.
- **Category 2 decline:** a price decline driven by deteriorating business fundamentals (the untradeable kind), vs Category 1 event overreaction (the tradeable kind).
- **Hold-through-print:** holding an options position through the earnings release itself, exposed to IV crush; banned by Tab 4 except ITM or flagged-binary positions.
- **AMC:** after market close.
- **IV crush:** the collapse in option implied volatility (and price) immediately after an earnings event resolves the uncertainty.
