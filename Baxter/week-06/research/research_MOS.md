## GLOSSARY

- **Range percentile:** Where a stock sits between its 52-week low (0%) and high (100%). Bottom quartile (≤25%) is the calls entry zone.
- **Rule 6 (Reachability):** The move required to reach option breakeven must be no more than 1.5x the stock's median earnings-day reaction, measured over its last 4-8 prints. Tests whether the dated catalyst can plausibly deliver the move, independent of whether the broader thesis is sound.
- **Day-0 move:** The stock's percentage price change on the trading day of the earnings release itself (as opposed to Day+1 or a multi-day drift).
- **Category 1 (event overreaction):** A decline caused by a dated shock that overshot the fundamentals — the only decline category Rule 5 of the unified screen treats as tradeable for calls.
- **Implied move:** The market-priced expected magnitude of a stock's move, read from at-the-money option pricing (straddle or IV). Forward-looking; distinct from Rule 6's backward-looking historical check.
- **Days-to-cover:** Shares short divided by average daily volume — how many trading days it would take short sellers to close out their positions at normal volume. Lower numbers mean less squeeze fuel stored up.
- **Form 4:** SEC filing for insider (officer/director) transactions in company stock, filed within 2 business days of the trade. Distinct from a 13F, which reports institutional fund holdings at quarter-end and is not an insider signal.
