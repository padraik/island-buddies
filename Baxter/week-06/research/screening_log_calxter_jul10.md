# SCREENING LOG: CALXTER, JULY 10 2026

Slice: the no-story control group. A systematic slice of the S&P MidCap 400 — every constituent whose ticker or company name starts with the letter M, in alphabetical order (by ticker symbol, matching the index's own listing order), continuing into N, until 50 unique names not on the excluded-universe list were reached. No narrative selection, no cherry-picking, no thesis. The point is base-rate statistics on where mechanically-chosen names die in the funnel, as a control against the story-driven slices the other Baxter agents are screening.

Universe-construction note: the real S&P MidCap 400 constituents starting with M total 21, of which 1 (MEDP) is on the exclusion list, leaving 20. Constituents starting with N total 16, none excluded. M+N together yield only 36 eligible names — short of the 50-name target the instructions anticipated. Per the instruction's own stated purpose (50 mechanically-selected names, no narrative judgment), the slice was extended alphabetically into O (still ticker-alphabetical order, still zero cherry-picking) to reach exactly 50: the first 14 O-tickers by symbol (OC through OSK) were added. This is disclosed here rather than silently substituting a smaller sample. Universe sourced from Wikipedia's "List of S&P 400 companies" (live-fetched July 10, 2026), cross-checked against two 2026 S&P Dow Jones rebalance announcements (Dec 2025, Mar 2026, Jun 2026 adds/deletes) which confirmed the snapshot's currency (e.g., MOG.A present, added Mar 2026; MASI absent, removed Jun 2026 for Sirius XM).

Method: WebSearch/WebFetch only, no Robinhood scripts, no other repo files. Screen implements lines 1-4 of the 13-line funnel (screen tier, not full DD) per the July 10 audit: (1) 52-week range percentile — bottom quartile = calls direction, top quartile = puts direction, middle = MID-OUT (killed, one search, move on); (2) confirmed earnings date inside an Aug/Sep 2026 expiry window, puts need 21+ DTE; (3) ratings direction filter (hardened): calls need max 1 Sell AND no downgrades/target cuts in 30 days; puts need min 2 Sell/Underperform ratings or a fresh Buy-to-Sell move within 30 days; (4) feasibility glance on Rule 5 (ask ≤$1.00 calls / ≤$1.50 puts) + Rule 6 (required move ≤1.5x typical earnings-day move). Midcap-specific: thin option chains (wide bid/ask, low open interest) are common across the S&P 400 — flagged as a Rule 5 concern on any ADVANCE where visible. Any unverifiable figure is marked UNVERIFIED. Thin-data names are marked DATA-INSUFFICIENT and passed over. MID-OUTs cost exactly one search each; only step-1 survivors get further searches. This is a screen, not DD — no deep dives.

**Reading the base rates:**

- **R1 (range percentile) kills 40% before any other check runs.** That's the cheapest and highest-leverage line in the funnel by design — it's a single search and it removed two-fifths of a purely mechanical, unthemed universe. No story-driven slice should expect to do worse than a 40% MID-OUT rate purely from range positioning; if a themed slice's MID-OUT rate is much lower than this, that's a signal the "story" is doing the selecting instead of the range check, which is exactly the failure mode Finding 1 warns about.
- **R3 (ratings direction) is the real kill zone: 44% of the full universe, 73% of everything that survived R1 (22 of 30).** This is the single largest mortality source in the entire funnel, well above R1 or R5/R6. Nearly every top-quartile name this screen found was riding fresh analyst upgrades and price-target raises (wrong direction for puts); nearly every bottom-quartile name was riding fresh downgrades and target cuts (wrong direction for calls, and exactly the "knife still falling" pattern Finding 2 flags). In a purely mechanical, no-thesis sample, momentum and analyst sentiment overwhelmingly point the *same* direction as the price trend, not against it — which is direct, unthemed evidence for Finding 1's core claim that momentum dominates mean-reversion at this time horizon. Two names (OHI, ORA) died on genuinely close judgment calls (one Sell short of the bar; a Sell-equivalent initiation inside the window) rather than clean fails, worth flagging for anyone auditing this log.
- **R5/R6 (feasibility) caught only 5 names (10%) because so few names got that far** — but of the 5 that did, 3 were confirmed structural fails (low-beta utilities/REITs, or a high-vol name whose chain was simply too rich), reinforcing Finding 1's mega-cap/low-vol exclusion logic even among mid-caps: boring dividend payers structurally cannot pay for a 6-week OTM option no matter how clean their ratings are.
- **Zero advances from 50 mechanically-selected names is itself the headline number.** For comparison, this control group should be read as the floor: any story-driven slice that also produces zero advances isn't necessarily doing anything wrong (this screen tier is supposed to be a hard filter), but a story-driven slice that advances names at a meaningfully higher rate than 0% should be able to explain *why* its selection process finds better setups than blind alphabetical order — otherwise the "story" may just be narrative selection bias dressed up as thesis, the exact concern this control group exists to catch.
- **DATA-INSUFFICIENT touched 8% of the full universe (4 of 50)** — two on earnings-date confirmation (MZTI, NSA), two on options-chain/historical-move confirmation (NXST, OLED). None were stretched into a guess; all were logged and passed over per the screen's data discipline.

## GLOSSARY

- **MID-OUT:** A name killed at line 1 of the funnel because its 52-week range percentile falls in the middle (roughly 25%-75%), qualifying it for neither a calls thesis (bottom quartile) nor a puts thesis (top quartile). Costs exactly one search and the name is dropped.
- **52-week range percentile:** Where a stock's current price sits between its 1-year low (0%) and high (100%). Bottom quartile (≤~25%) sorts toward calls; top quartile (≥~75%) sorts toward puts; the middle is MID-OUT. Rule 1 of the 13-line funnel.
- **R1 / R2 / R3 / R5 / R6:** Shorthand for the numbered lines of the July 10 audit's consolidated 13-line funnel. R1 = range percentile. R2 = confirmed earnings inside the Aug/Sep expiry window (21+ DTE for puts). R3 = the hardened ratings direction filter (calls: max 1 Sell and no downgrades/target cuts in 30 days; puts: min 2 Sell/Underperform ratings or a fresh Buy-to-Sell move in 30 days). R5 = the options-chain feasibility check (ask ≤$1.00 for calls, ≤$1.50 for puts). R6 = Rule 6 reachability (required move to the strike must be ≤1.5x the stock's typical historical earnings-day move). This screen implements lines 1-4 of the funnel only (the "screen tier"); lines 5-13 are full-DD-only and were never reached by any name in this batch.
- **DTE:** Days to expiration.
- **Analyst capitulation:** The pattern of analysts cutting ratings/targets only after a decline is already underway, rather than ahead of it. A stock at lows with fresh downgrades in the trailing 30 days is read as capitulation still in progress, not completed — a disqualifying pattern under the hardened Rule 3 (see Finding 2 of the July 10 audit).
- **Floor freshness:** A DD-tier concept (line 6 of the 13-line funnel, not reached by this screen) requiring that any bear-floor/bull-ceiling price target be dated within 60 days and published after the stock's decline, so a stale pre-decline target isn't mistaken for real support.
- **Implied move:** The total price move the options market is pricing for an event, read off the near-expiry straddle. A DD-tier cross-check (line 9), not part of the screen tier.
- **Required move:** The percentage move a stock needs to make to reach a given option strike (i.e., to approach breakeven). Compared against 1.5x the stock's typical historical earnings-day move under Rule 6.
- **Beta:** A measure of a stock's volatility relative to the broad market (beta of 1.0 = moves with the market; below 1.0 = historically calmer; above 1.0 = historically more volatile). Used in this log as a quick proxy for whether a low-vol name could plausibly clear Rule 6.
- **Thin chain liquidity:** Wide bid/ask spreads and low open interest in an options chain, flagged as a Rule 5 concern specific to mid-cap names (as opposed to the deep, liquid chains typical of mega-caps), per the house note on this screen's brief. Not directly observed on any name in this batch since none reached a fully-priced ADVANCE, but noted as a standing risk for any future DD on a mid-cap name that does clear the screen.
- **DATA-INSUFFICIENT:** This screen's convention for a figure that could not be confirmed via WebSearch/WebFetch after a reasonable attempt (typically 1-2 searches). The name is killed at whatever line the missing data blocked and the screen moves on, rather than guessing.
- **UNVERIFIED:** This screen's convention for a figure found in only one low-confidence source and not cross-checked; used to flag rather than discard a number. (Not triggered in this batch — conflicting-source cases here were instead resolved with a second search or disclosed as an explicit range/conflict, e.g. NVST, NVT, OLN, OSK.)
