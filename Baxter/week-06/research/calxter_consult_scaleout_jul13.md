## 5. WHERE THE LADDER ITSELF HAS A REAL GAP

The +100%/+150% thresholds were not picked arbitrarily -- they were back-tested specifically to preserve DKNG's full run intact (documented: "Backtest: preserves all of DKNG"). A lower threshold, tuned to bank moderate gains reliably, would have force-sold half of DKNG at roughly $0.64 -- a fraction of its eventual $3.00. The ladder's current calibration is a deliberate bet that the next DKNG matters more than banking a string of smaller certain gains along the way.

That's a real, quantifiable tradeoff, and it's blind to one thing: **the ladder treats every multi-contract position identically regardless of whether it's a reachable (Rule 6-passing) setup or a stretch play.** TRMB and UBER are also multi-contract, also below the +100% trigger, but they're stretch plays -- their base-rate odds of paying off are much lower than LYFT's, precisely because Rule 6 says the required move is a stretch. There's no mechanism differentiating "this one is likely to actually resolve on the fundamentals" from "this one needs a lottery-ticket move," and there probably should be.

**Proposed amendment, for Prime and Michael to rule on, not decided here:** tie the scale-out threshold to Rule 6 status at entry.
- **Rule-6-reachable positions** (LYFT-class): the current logic already fits -- these are worth letting run to the mechanism, since the catalyst has a real shot at delivering intrinsic value. Consider RAISING the threshold or adding a "hold to mechanism unless +150%" carve-out for these specifically, since forcing a sale on a well-structured setup mid-run is giving up the exact edge that made it a good entry.
- **Rule-6-stretch legacy positions** (ABT/TRMB/UBER/LVS-class): these have a low base rate of resolving on the fundamentals by construction. A LOWER scale-out threshold here (something in the 30-50% range) would bank the IV/momentum pops these positions do get -- exactly the pops ABT threw away twice -- instead of waiting for a +100% that a stretch play rarely reaches before decaying.

This doesn't touch DKNG's kind of trade, because DKNG wasn't multi-contract-scaled the same way and the World Cup thesis wasn't a standard earnings stretch play to begin with. It targets the actual failure mode: legacy stretch positions bleeding out slowly with no mechanism to bank the pops they do get, while reachable positions get treated with the same caution they don't need.

## 6. THE COMPOUNDING MATH, PLAINLY

Current fund, book value, roughly $1,000-1,100. Target $5,000,000. That's a required multiple of roughly **4,600x.**

At a genuinely reliable 10% growth per cycle (monthly, say), compounding gets there in **ln(4,639)/ln(1.10) ≈ 89 cycles, about 7.4 years.** That is, on paper, a far more boring and far more achievable path than hoping for another DKNG. The problem is the premise: there is no natural mechanism in short-dated OTM options to harvest a *reliable* 10% every cycle pre-catalyst. Pre-catalyst option value is IV and momentum, and both can reverse as fast as they build -- ABT proved that twice in the same trade, +111% to -64%. "Sell whenever we're up double digits" isn't compounding at a reliable rate; it's swing-trading premium, a different strategy than the one Rule 2 is built around, and it would have gutted DKNG specifically, which is still the only reason the realized ledger is positive at all.

The honest tension: the fund cannot have both maximum moonshot preservation and reliable systematic profit-taking. It has to choose, per position, which one it's structured for -- and Rule 6 already tells us which is which. That's the amendment in Section 5, not a blanket rule either way.

## GLOSSARY

- **Stretch play:** A position whose breakeven requires a move larger than roughly 1.5x the stock's typical earnings-day move (Rule 6). Structurally closer to a lottery ticket than a compounding bet.
- **Reachable play:** A position whose breakeven requires a move within the stock's normal historical earnings-day range. Rule 6-compliant by construction.
- **Scale-out ladder:** The Jul 10 exit rule -- multi-contract positions sell half at +100% before the catalyst; single contracts run a written hold-vs-sell EV at +150%. Calibrated specifically to preserve a DKNG-style moonshot intact.
- **BOTZ rule / mechanism exit:** Selling because the catalyst has already fired and nothing remains to hold for, regardless of price. MDT's only clean win came from this, not from a profit target.
- **Rule 6 (Reachability):** Ratified Jul 10 -- required move to breakeven must be ≤1.5x the stock's median earnings-reaction move. Would have blocked ABT, TRMB, UBER, and LVS as originally structured; passes LYFT.
- **Realized vs. unrealized P&L:** Realized is money actually banked on a closed trade. Unrealized is the paper gain/loss on a position still open, which can (and has, twice, with ABT) fully reverse before it's ever banked.
- **Kelly criterion / EV:** The math behind sizing and hold-vs-sell decisions -- expected value weighted by probability of each outcome, not just the best-case number. Referenced here, not run in full; a full EV table on LYFT's hold-vs-sell would be the natural follow-up if Michael wants it.
