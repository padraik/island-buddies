## GLOSSARY

- **52-week range percentile:** Where a stock's current price sits between its 1-year low (0%) and 1-year high (100%). Bottom quartile (0-25%) is the calls hunting ground; top quartile (75-100%) is the puts hunting ground; the middle is MID-OUT (screened out, not our directional edge).
- **MID-OUT:** Screen-tier verdict for a name whose range percentile falls in the middle (roughly 25-75%) - neither dislocated toward a low nor a high, so it offers no range-based edge for either calls or puts and is killed at a glance.
- **DTE:** Days to expiration.
- **R1-R6 (funnel line numbers):** Shorthand for the numbered screen/DD steps in the Jul 10 audit's thirteen-line funnel. R1 = range percentile, R2 = confirmed earnings inside the expiry window, R3 = ratings direction filter, R4 = feasibility glance (this log also uses "R5-R6" loosely to mean the Rule 5 chain-affordability / Rule 6 reachability feasibility glance done at R4), R5 = full chain filter (DD-only), R6 = reachability table (DD-only).
- **Ratings direction filter (R3, hardened):** For calls, max 1 Sell-type rating AND no downgrades or price-target cuts in the trailing 30 days. For puts, minimum 2 Sell/Underperform ratings or a fresh Buy-to-Sell downgrade. Replaces the old "max 1 Sell" rule that Finding 2 showed selects for stocks whose analyst capitulation is still ahead of them, not behind them.
- **Feasibility glance (R4):** A quick gut-check on whether a name could plausibly clear Rule 5 (an OTM strike with an ask no higher than $1.00 for calls / $1.50 for puts) AND Rule 6 (the move required to reach breakeven is no more than 1.5x the stock's typical earnings-day move) at the same time, without pulling the actual options chain. Expensive, low-volatility mega-caps (e.g., a $95 stock that moves 2-3% on earnings) fail this at a glance and are killed before any chain data is pulled.
- **Decline category (Finding 4):** Every bottom-quartile name is sorted into one of four buckets before it can proceed. Only category 1 is tradeable with calls:
  1. **Event overreaction** - a dated shock (guidance cut, one bad print, litigation/M&A speculation) knocked the stock down more than the underlying fundamentals moved; recoverable, with a catalyst to attach the recovery to. The only tradeable category.
  2. **Secular decline** - the business is structurally losing ground (share loss, subscriber erosion, margin compression that isn't reversing); the low keeps getting lower regardless of any single print.
  3. **Macro beta** - the stock is down because its sector or the broader tape is down (e.g., a sector-wide valuation reset, a REIT-sector rate re-rating); no idiosyncratic catalyst is coming on earnings day to fix what the sector did.
  4. **Commodity/cycle unwind** - the decline tracks a commodity price or industry cycle (energy, tariff-driven input costs) normalizing, not a one-time dislocation.
- **Out of lane:** Killed not on the funnel's merits but because the name falls into a hunting ground assigned to a different screening agent or explicitly excluded from this slice: high-short-interest/meme-adjacent retail names, policy- or regulatory-driven names (active antitrust/litigation overhangs), and S&P 400 mid-cap "M-names."
- **DATA-INSUFFICIENT:** Verdict used when available search results could not confirm a name's current price, range position, or other screen-critical figure with reasonable confidence; rather than guess, the name is killed and logged as thin-data.
- **UNVERIFIED:** A specific figure (price, date, rating count) that could not be confirmed via live search but where enough surrounding data existed to still render a screen verdict; the figure is flagged rather than presented as fact.
- **Straddle-implied move / short interest / days-to-cover / Form 4 / Kelly criterion:** DD-tier concepts (funnel lines 5-13) not evaluated at this screen tier; see the Jul 10 selection-criteria audit's own glossary for definitions. Not re-defined here because no screened name in this log reached them.
