## GLOSSARY

- **ITM (in-the-money):** A call whose strike sits below the current stock price, so part of the premium is intrinsic value rather than pure time value. Rarer in this fund's screens because ITM strikes usually breach the $1.00 Rule 5 cap; BTG's low share price makes it affordable.
- **AISC (all-in sustaining cost):** The gold-mining industry's standard per-ounce cost measure including sustaining capex. BTG's 2026 guide of $2,400-2,580/oz is transition-year elevated.
- **Rule 6 (Reachability):** Required breakeven move must be ≤1.5x the stock's median earnings-day reaction over its last 4-8 prints. Tests the clock, not the thesis.
- **Decline categories:** (1) event overreaction — only tradeable class; (2) secular decline; (3) macro/chronic risk beta; (4) commodity cycle unwind. Named with evidence before any entry.
- **Bear floor (Rule 4):** The lowest Buy-rated analyst target must clear the option breakeven, on a target dated within 60 days and published post-decline.
- **6-K:** SEC filing form for foreign private issuers' current reports — B2Gold (Canadian) announces earnings dates and results on 6-Ks rather than 8-Ks.
- **Deferred stripping:** Mine-sequencing capex where waste rock removal is front-loaded ahead of ore access — inflates near-term AISC, normalizes later.
