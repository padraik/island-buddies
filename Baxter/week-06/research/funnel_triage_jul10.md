## GLOSSARY

- **Funnel lines 1-13:** The upgraded selection sequence ratified July 10 (see selection_criteria_audit_jul10.md). Lines 1-4 are the screen tier; lines 5-13 are DD.
- **Line 5 / decline category:** Every candidate must have its decline named: (1) event overreaction, (2) secular decline, (3) macro beta, (4) commodity/cycle unwind. Only category 1 is tradeable with calls.
- **Rule 5 (chain filter):** Option ask at or below $1.00/share for calls, $1.50 for puts. A thesis without an affordable instrument is not a play.
- **Rule 6 (reachability):** Required move to breakeven must be at or below 1.5x the stock's median earnings-reaction move. Validates the clock, not the thesis.
- **BOTZ rule:** Themes without dated data mechanisms are dead money. Named for Sheldon's robotics ETF.
- **Breakeven:** Strike plus premium (calls) or strike minus premium (puts). The stock price at which the option is worth what was paid at expiry.
- **IV (implied volatility):** The market's priced expectation of movement, embedded in option premiums. Rich IV inflates asks and can make Rule 5 and Rule 6 mutually unsatisfiable (UPST).
- **Dilution treadmill:** A company funding operations by repeatedly issuing shares, structurally depressing the stock. Category 2, never an overreaction.
- **Days to cover (DTC):** Short interest divided by average daily volume; how long shorts need to buy back. A squeeze-fuel gauge.
- **Puts protocol M&A disqualifier:** Any takeover rumor, strategic review, or activist campaign disqualifies a puts play. A deal announcement gaps the stock up with no exit.
- **Back-test gate:** Standing decision: no live puts entry until the passes.md back-test is run and documented. Deadline Jul 31.
