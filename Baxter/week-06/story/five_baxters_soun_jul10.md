# THE FIVE BAXTERS: SOUN
*Friday night, July 10, 2026. Hours after the audit. The new machine is still warm.*

---

The screeners came home at dusk. Two hundred and two names went out; the four of them dragged back eleven, and by the time Prime finished with the chains there was one name on the whiteboard and ten crossed out beneath it, each with its line number written next to the strike-through like a coroner's note.

Calxter spoke first, which was unusual, but he had earned it. His fifty mechanical names had gone zero for fifty.

**CALXTER:** For the record: the control group died at the ratings filter forty-four percent of the time. Top-quartile names ride fresh upgrades. Bottom-quartile names ride fresh downgrades. Both wrong-direction. The unthemed base rate for surviving our own screen is zero in fifty. Whatever advances from a themed slice is either signal or selection bias, and the difference is everything.

**BULLXTER:** It's signal. Look at it. Fourth percentile of the range. Second-most-shorted stock in America over two billion. Forty percent of the float is borrowed and sold by people who have to buy it back through a door that's four point nine days wide. Core business growing eighty-eight percent. And the chain is selling the August seven-strike for sixty-three cents. Sixty-three cents! The audit told us this morning what pays a call in six weeks: a gap, made of forced buying. This is a warehouse of forced buying with a dated fuse.

**BEARXTER:** Now say what it is instead of what it rhymes with. This company loses twenty-five million dollars a quarter, and in April it printed new shares to buy LivePerson, a declining business, with paper. The market didn't overreact to that. The market *reacted* to it. Dilution is not sentiment; it is arithmetic. I want it on the record that category one is Prime's call tonight, not mine.

**PRIME:** It's on the record. Here's why it's still category one: the event is dated, April. The response since has been indiscriminate. They sold a fifty-two percent revenue beat twelve percent in May. When a market sells accelerating fundamentals because of a four-month-old headline, that is overhang, and overhang is the thing earnings prints resolve. Your dissent is in the doc, in your words.

**BEARXTER:** Then here are my conditions, also in my words. Line eleven is naked. Nobody in this room knows which way estimates revised in the last thirty days, and the last print was a miss, by a penny, but a miss. So: revision direction gets verified before any order exists, and if it's falling, this is a three-and-a-half, and the cap queues it behind TRMB and LYFT, where it can sit and think about what it almost was. Second: someone checks the LivePerson closing calendar. We are not holding a dilution event inside our own window and calling it bad luck. Third: bottom of the four-five tier. Twelve percent, not sixteen. One contract.

**CALXTER:** The arithmetic agrees with the caution, which is rare enough to note. Twelve percent of five twenty-seven is $63 and change. The ask is $0.63. One contract, no rounding, no debate about halves. And the cross-check, since it's mine: the August-seven straddle prices a twenty-five percent move. We require fifteen point one. We are buying a move the market itself calls ordinary, at the bottom of the tier, with the tail thrown in. The EV is positive under weights Bearxter chose.

**MACXTER:** Nothing on my desk touches it. No tariff exposure that matters at this size, no filing overhang beyond the deal itself. My clock note is the only one: the catalyst is August sixth, we'd enter July thirteenth, and under the new Tab 4 this position is *sold* on August fourth or fifth into the ramp. The print is the destination of the premium, not of the position. If anyone in this room catches anyone else arguing to hold through that print because it's "already up," the audit gets read aloud. All of it.

**BULLXTER:** Fine. But I want MY line on the record too, the one from tonight's funnel: thirteen yeses used to buy a lottery ticket, now they buy a good one. This name gave us nine yeses, one neutral, one gate, and the two softest lines still pass on their conservative reads. When the gate clears, nobody gets timid about the $63. It's the right $63.

**PRIME:** Then the verdict. Four of five, sized at twelve percent, one contract, August twenty-one seven-strike, limit $0.63, tolerance to $0.75. Entry conditions: revisions verified flat or up, deal calendar clear through August fifth, stock under $7 at the order. Exit: the ramp, August fourth or fifth, per Tab 4. ITM is the only exception, and it isn't ours to invoke tonight. And Michael's yes, which is a rule older than all six of the others.

*The binder closed. Somewhere in the house, the modem was quiet for once, and the whiteboard held one name and ten crossed out, which, as Calxter observed on his way out, was exactly the ratio the base rates said an honest funnel should produce.*
