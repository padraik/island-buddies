For five years, the bear thesis on the company was simple: the industry runs on promotions and free bets, customer acquisition costs exceed lifetime value, the business will never generate real profit.

In Q1 2026, DraftKings reported GAAP net income of $21.1 million. Positive. Not adjusted. Not EBITDA. Not "path to." GAAP net income, under the same accounting rules that govern every public company.

The bear thesis required that number to be negative. It was not negative.

At the same time: the stock sat at $25.38. The 52-week high was $48.78. Every analyst who covered the name -- 29 in total -- rated it a Buy or Strong Buy. The consensus price target was $34-37, roughly 35-46% above the stock price. The company had $647 million in free cash flow and debt at less than one times EBITDA.

The largest global sporting event in the world was happening in their backyard, and they would book the handle from 25 states. On July 31, management would report Q2 with the complete World Cup data in hand -- every game, every bet, every new depositor.

## GLOSSARY

**GAAP net income:** The "bottom line" profit calculated under Generally Accepted Accounting Principles. Includes all expenses, taxes, interest, and non-cash charges. No adjustments for "one-time items" or stock compensation. DraftKings' $21.1M GAAP net income in Q1 2026 was the number that ended the bear thesis -- bears required it to be negative, and it was positive.

**Sports betting handle:** Total amount wagered through the platform. DraftKings earns a percentage (the "hold rate") of all handle. More handle = more revenue. World Cup 2026 represented the largest soccer betting event in DraftKings' history, with 25-state coverage and a new soccer-specific betting product.

**Hold rate:** The percentage of total handle the sportsbook retains as revenue. DraftKings' Q1 2026 hold rate was 7.8%, up from 6.4% the prior year. Each percentage point improvement on billions in handle equals significant incremental revenue without requiring new customers.

**Bear thesis (sports betting):** The five-year argument against sports betting stocks: customer acquisition costs exceed lifetime value, promotional spending is structural, the industry will never generate real GAAP profit. This thesis was widely held and had been correct for years. Q1 2026 GAAP profitability at DraftKings broke it.

**BOTZ rule:** Named after an ETF that Michael referenced when explaining that themes without near-term data catalysts lose value over time as they wait for the next signal. Applied here: between World Cup Day 2 and the July 31 earnings call, there was no scheduled mechanism to push DKNG higher. Sell into the open window rather than hold through dead time.

**World Cup 2026:** FIFA Men's World Cup, hosted in the United States, Mexico, and Canada. June 11 -- July 19. 104 games, 16 US cities. DraftKings had 25 state licenses active and a soccer-specific betting product built specifically for the tournament. The Group Stage ran June 11-30, providing maximum betting volume early in the window.

**Iron Rules:** Five screening criteria for every Island Fund position. This play passed all five: (1) stock in bottom 20% of range, (2) earnings catalyst in window, (3) near-zero Sell ratings, (4) bear floor above breakeven, (5) option under $1.00. The $27.5C at $0.49 cleared every criterion.
