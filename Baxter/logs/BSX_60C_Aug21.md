## THE SETUP

Boston Scientific makes the devices that fix hearts. The WATCHMAN is their flagship: a small implant that seals the left atrial appendage in patients with atrial fibrillation, preventing blood clots from causing strokes. The company also makes electrophysiology catheters, urology tools, and endoscopy equipment.

On April 22, 2026, Boston Scientific cut its full-year guidance. Revenue growth from 10-11% down to 7-8.5%. EPS from $3.57 guidance down to $3.34-3.41. The reason: WATCHMAN procedure volumes declining faster than expected, and the electrophysiology segment slowing as new ablation technologies competed for the same patients.

The stock fell approximately 57% from $109 to $47.

On June 11, 2026 -- six weeks after the guidance cut -- the stock was sitting at $47.40. The 52-week range ran from $47.35 to $109.50. The stock was 0.04% from its 52-week floor. Not approximately at the low. Not near the low. On the low.

Twenty-two analysts cover Boston Scientific. Zero have a Sell rating. The most pessimistic -- Truist at $64, BTIG at $65 -- had bear floors that were still $3.27 and $4.27 above the breakeven of $60.73.

Even the most cautious analysts thought the April guidance cut was not a death sentence. They had already updated their models to reflect the new guidance. They had already heard management's explanation of the WATCHMAN slowdown. And after reviewing all of it, the most pessimistic one said $64.

## THE STORY

The debate inside the five-Baxter meeting was direct. Bearxter's concern was the guidance cut: not that it was an overreaction, but that it represented new information about a flagship product (WATCHMAN volumes declining) that the market had not previously priced. That type of information -- a genuine data point about a product the bulls had been counting on -- can be a one-time revision or the first signal of a structural problem.

Bearxter's conditional approval came down to a single question: would July 29 produce a second guidance cut?

If yes: thesis broken, loss accepted, lesson written.

If no: the April guidance cut was a timing issue rather than a structural one. Q2 would show stabilization. The stock would re-rate from trough toward the analyst floors.

Bullxter's counterpoint was also direct. Twenty-two analysts with access to management, to channel data, to competitive intelligence, and to the WATCHMAN procedural volume data had all updated their models after the guidance cut. After updating, the most pessimistic one said $64. Not $47. Not $40. Sixty-four dollars. They knew about the WATCHMAN issue. They knew about the electrophysiology competition. And they still said $64.

The breakeven of $60.73 was below every analyst's floor. That is what the research called "the cleanest criterion." When the bears are above the breakeven, you don't need to be bullish to be right. You just need the bears to be right.

The position was entered. $70 invested. July 29 was the answer.

## GLOSSARY

**WATCHMAN:** Boston Scientific's flagship left atrial appendage closure device. Implanted in patients with atrial fibrillation who cannot tolerate long-term blood thinners, it prevents blood clots from causing strokes. Declining procedure volumes were the primary reason for the April 2026 guidance cut.

**Electrophysiology (EP):** Cardiology subspecialty focused on the heart's electrical system. Ablation procedures -- which use heat, cold, or energy to destroy abnormal heart tissue -- compete with WATCHMAN as treatment for AFib. Newer pulsed field ablation technology has drawn patients away from WATCHMAN procedures in some markets.

**Guidance cut:** Boston Scientific's April 22, 2026 revision of full-year growth expectations from 10-11% to 7-8.5%. The stock fell roughly 57% on and after this announcement, pricing in not just the guidance miss but also the market's uncertainty about whether additional cuts would follow.

**Rule 4 (entry):** Iron Rule requiring that the bear floor (lowest Buy/Outperform analyst target) be above the option's breakeven at the time of entry. If the most pessimistic credible analyst says the stock is worth more than our breakeven, we only need the pessimists to be right to make money.

**Rule 4 (live position):** The exit rule added after BSX. If any analyst on a live position updates their target to below the option's breakeven at any point during the holding period, exit the same day. The entry condition must hold throughout. The condition exists to protect against exactly the scenario where the information landscape shifts while the position is open.

**Bear floor:** The lowest analyst price target from Buy or Outperform analysts. At BSX entry: Truist $64, BTIG $65 -- both above our $60.73 breakeven. After the June 12 cut to $55: the new bear floor was below breakeven, and the position was no longer structured correctly.
