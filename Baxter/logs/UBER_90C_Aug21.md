## THE SETUP

Uber Technologies runs the largest rideshare and food delivery network on earth. Fifty million Uber One members. Gross bookings growing 25% year over year. Q1 2026: beat estimates by $900 million, guided Q2 above consensus, EPS up 44%. And the stock was sitting at $72 -- at 11% of its 52-week range.

The reason the stock was at $72 on a beat-and-guide-up quarter: analysts cut full-year EPS estimates by 12% based on revised operating expense projections. The revenue estimate did not move. The market repriced on EPS, not on bookings. The business was being valued at its most conservative margin forecast while posting the kind of gross bookings growth that other tech companies would announce with confetti.

Forty-five analysts rated Uber a Buy. Zero rated it Sell. The average target: $104. The only analyst with a sub-$90 target was Wedbush -- at $75, Neutral rating, excluded from the bear floor. The lowest Buy-rated analyst was comfortably above the breakeven.

The World Cup was running: June 11 through July 19. Uber was the official mobility partner. World Cup ride packages, Uber One priority access, delivery bundles. Management had made specific product offerings for the tournament. The stock was at $72.

Then on June 16-17, the FOMC came in hawkish. Kevin Warsh removed cut-bias language. The dot plot shifted toward a possible hike. Rate-sensitive sectors sold off. UBER fell with the broad tech tape and sat at $72.03 as of June 18 -- essentially unchanged from June 5 when the original research was filed.

Then the Iran deal was announced.

## THE STORY

The original research was filed June 5 with a July 17 expiry and 4/5 conviction. The instrument was the $85C Jul17. All five Iron Rules passed.

The entry was held because capital was committed elsewhere. The plan was June 19.

June 18 arrived with two events: the Iran deal announcement and a review of whether the July 17 window still made sense.

It did not.

The World Cup had been running for seven days. UBER was flat. If the market was going to re-rate on tournament volume, it would have started. The confirmation of World Cup demand would come at Q2 earnings on August 4 -- which was outside the July 17 window. The market prices confirmed data, not inferred demand. Entering the July 17 window meant betting the market would re-rate on what it had just spent seven days not reacting to.

Macxter had flagged the Iran deal: oil prices falling as Strait of Hormuz reopens. Cheaper crude = lower cost structure for rideshare operations. Driver incentives hold better when gas is cheaper. Consumer willingness to use Uber vs. drive their own car improves when filling a tank costs less. The deal was announced the same morning UBER was being reviewed.

The solution was the expiry, not the conviction. Switch from July 17 to August 21. Q2 earnings on August 4 land inside the August 21 window. The catalyst becomes a binary event on a specific date -- the same structure that made ABT and TRMB clean entries. The World Cup becomes a pre-earnings tailwind rather than the entire thesis.

The $90C Aug21 was asking $0.73 at time of review. The chain showed it as the lowest-breakeven option that passed Rule 5.

## GLOSSARY

**Gross Bookings:** Total dollar value of all trips and orders processed through Uber's platform before Uber takes its commission. Growing 25% year over year in Q1 2026. The revenue estimate did not move when analysts cut EPS -- meaning the market still believes the volume is coming, just that Uber is temporarily spending more to capture it.

**EPS revision:** After Q1, analysts cut full-year EPS estimates from $3.37 to $2.95 (a 12% reduction) based on revised operating expense projections, particularly driver incentives and autonomous vehicle investment. This is what repriced the stock from $100 to $72 -- not the revenue, not the bookings, not the guidance. The margin forecast.

**Uber One:** Paid subscription program. 50 million members representing approximately half of gross bookings. Members get priority access during high-demand windows like the World Cup. The membership base is the demand layer that makes Uber's World Cup product initiative real.

**Bear floor:** Lowest price target among Buy/Outperform analysts only. Wedbush at $75 (Neutral/Hold) is excluded. With 45 Buy analysts averaging $104, the bear floor is well above the $90.65 breakeven. Rule 4 passes.

**Iran deal (June 18):** US-Iran agreement to end hostilities and reopen the Strait of Hormuz. Iranian crude re-enters the market. Oil prices fall. Rideshare unit economics improve: cheaper gas means more drivers online, lower driver incentive spend, and consumers more willing to use rideshare vs. driving themselves.

**World Cup 2026:** June 11 -- July 19 in the United States. Uber is the official mobility partner. The tournament demand has been running for 7 days at entry without moving the stock -- consistent with the market waiting for Q2 confirmation (August 4) rather than repricing on inferred volume.

**Autonomous vehicle (AV) investment:** Uber committed $10 billion to build a robotaxi fleet and partner with Rivian and Lucid. The investment period is now. The margin payoff is 3-7 years out. This is the reason EPS estimates got cut -- investment spending hitting the income statement before the fleet generates revenue.
