## THE SETUP

Descartes Systems Group builds global trade compliance software. Their product is called Global Trade Intelligence -- a platform that contains tariff classifications, customs rules, duty rates, and compliance requirements for over 200 countries. Every time the US government announces a new tariff schedule, every time a trade agreement is modified, every time customs regulations change in any major country, Descartes' platform has to be updated. And their customers pay for those updates on subscription.

In an era when tariff policy was changing faster than at any point in a generation, Descartes was the beneficiary. The complexity was their product.

The stock was at $74 on June 1 -- at 10% of its 52-week range. The 52-week ran from $62.56 to $115.88. The analyst consensus was $100.85 -- 36% above the stock price. Eight analysts said Buy. One said Hold. Zero said Sell.

The problem was the instrument. The only viable chain had a $85C Jun18 at $0.80, with a breakeven of $85.80. The lone Hold analyst had a target of $82. The breakeven was $3.80 above the most pessimistic analyst's target. Rule 4 failed at entry.

The thesis was filed as a WATCH: if the Hold analyst revised their target upward following the earnings print, the instrument would become viable.

On June 3 after close: Descartes reported Q1 FY2027. Revenue up 15% year over year -- a record quarter. Net income up 34%. The tariff complexity thesis was in the numbers. Management cited Global Trade Intelligence growth directly. The stock fell 2.89% after hours, to $71.97 -- a market processing guidance that was slightly soft on an excellent quarter. A modest reaction compared to peers. VEEV fell 6% on a similar structure; DSGX fell 3%.

The research flag was: if the Hold analyst revises to $88 or above, pull the chain. At $88, the bear floor would be above the $85.80 breakeven by $2.20 -- sufficient margin. Entry viable.

The Hold analyst had not yet revised. Bear floor was still $82. The breakeven of $90.45 was $8.45 above the bear floor. Rule 4 still failed.

The entry was made anyway.

## GLOSSARY

**Global Trade Intelligence:** Descartes' data platform containing tariff classifications, duty rates, customs regulations, and compliance requirements for 200+ countries. Revenue is subscription-based. Every new tariff schedule or trade policy change generates platform updates that existing customers pay to access.

**Rule 4 failure (at entry):** A structural condition where the most pessimistic analyst's target is below the option's breakeven. Entering with Rule 4 failing means accepting a trade that requires the bears to be wrong AND the stock to move the required amount in the available time. The system was designed to filter this out. Entering anyway is overriding the system.

**Post-earnings entry:** Buying an option after a company has reported earnings, when the stock has already reacted to the news. In theory, post-earnings entries avoid IV crush and provide the ability to react to confirmed information. In practice, the remaining time window is short, and the setup must pass all Iron Rules -- including Rule 4 -- to be viable.

**Gap-and-fade (DSGX version):** Unlike HITI, which spiked 40%+ in after-hours, DSGX's after-hours reaction was mild (-3%). The gap-and-fade here was smaller -- the stock bounced modestly at open on June 4 then fell back. Short-dated OTM options on a stock with modest movement cannot recover their premium without a large sustained directional move.

**BOTZ rule (application):** Even if the catalyst fires (Q1 earnings) and the result is positive (record quarter), the BOTZ rule applies to the option: if the position is not tracking toward the breakeven in the remaining time window, exit. Themes (great business, tariff tailwind) without near-term data catalysts (no scheduled event in the June 18 window) are dead money.

**Bear floor:** Lowest analyst price target among Buy/Outperform analysts. For DSGX: $82 (Hold analyst). Breakeven: $90.45. Gap: -$8.45. The trade required the bears to be wrong by $8.45 on a stock with 14 days remaining. That is not the Iron Rules structure.
