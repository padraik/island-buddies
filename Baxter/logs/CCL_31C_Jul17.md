## THE SETUP

Carnival Corporation is the largest cruise line in the world. If you have ever taken a cruise on a ship with a funnel painted red and white, there is a good chance it was theirs or one of their subsidiaries: Costa, Holland America, Princess, Cunard. By the time of this entry, the company had navigated through COVID shutdowns, the slowest recovery in consumer travel, and a balance sheet loaded with debt from those years. The recovery was real and ongoing.

The numbers on June 1 were interesting. Net profit up 50.9% year over year. Twenty-one of twenty-six analysts bullish. Zero Sell ratings. The stock was at roughly $30 -- at 45% of its 52-week range, not at its low, but not near its high.

The complication: the most pessimistic analyst on the stock had a target of $28.70. That is below the $31.99 breakeven on the $31C Jul17. The bears said the stock was worth $28.70. We needed it to reach $31.99 to make any money. That is a structural gap against us.

The meeting debated STZ and CCL as the two candidates after the fund had committed to NKE, ABT, and HITI in the first deployment. The room chose STZ originally -- the World Cup Modelo thesis was more specific, more dated, and more directly tied to a near-term catalyst. But when the STZ instrument became unavailable at the right price, CCL was the available play.

3.5/5 conviction. One contract. $99 at risk.

## THE STORY

The position was entered knowing the bear gap existed. The room had acknowledged it directly: Truist's bear case at $28.70 was $3.29 below the breakeven. The counterargument was that the analyst trend was moving upward -- Loop Capital had initiated coverage at $36 the same day, and the direction of coverage was bullish even if the floor was low.

Carnival earnings were June 23. In the window before the call, the stock would need to sustain the current level and then re-rate on the earnings print. Fifty percent profit growth was the floor expectation -- not a heroic beat, just confirmation of the recovery thesis.

Then the stock started moving down.

The specific trigger: the stock fell below the $28.70 bear analyst floor. Not a guidance cut, not a new piece of news -- the market moved the price below the level where even the most cautious analyst thought the stock belonged.

When a stock trades below the most pessimistic credible analyst's fair value estimate, something has changed that the analyst has not yet accounted for -- or the market is wrong in a way that will correct itself, but either way, the original entry condition is broken. The thesis was built on a bear floor of $28.70. The stock was now below $28.70.

Rule 4 breach. Exit.

Sold at $1.00. Recovered $100. Net P&L: +$1.

## GLOSSARY

**Bear Floor Gap:** The distance between the most pessimistic analyst's price target and the option's breakeven. For ABT, the gap was positive ($106 floor vs. $100.78 breakeven = +$5.22 in our favor). For CCL, the gap was negative ($28.70 floor vs. $31.99 breakeven = -$3.29 against us). A negative gap means the bears have to be wrong for the trade to make money.

**Fifty percent profit growth:** Carnival's net profit grew 50.9% year over year -- a genuine business recovery. This number passed the fundamental screen. The structural gap on the options chain did not.

**Rule 4 breach:** The stock traded below the lowest credible analyst target ($28.70). When the market is pricing the stock below the most pessimistic analyst's fair value estimate, the basis for the trade's risk/reward structure is gone. Exit.

**Zero Sell ratings:** One of the screening conditions. Twenty-six analysts covered CCL. Zero had Sell or Underperform ratings. This means the full analyst community was either bullish or neutral. It does not, by itself, mean the option's structure was correct -- just that there was no institutional-grade bear thesis.

**3.5/5 conviction:** The Island Fund's internal rating indicating the fundamental thesis is valid but the instrument or structure has a meaningful weakness. In CCL's case: valid business recovery thesis, valid analyst consensus, zero Sell ratings -- but bear floor below breakeven created structural vulnerability. Not a 4/5 because the structure did not pass all Iron Rules cleanly.

**Rotation:** Exiting one position and deploying the freed capital into a higher-conviction opportunity. CCL's exit freed capital for MDT's post-earnings entry, which returned +42%.
