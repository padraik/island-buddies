## THE SETUP

Abbott Laboratories makes the equipment and tests that keep people alive. Their flagship product in 2026 is FreeStyle Libre, a continuous glucose monitor worn on the arm that measures blood sugar every minute without a finger stick. Before that it was COVID tests. Before that, decades of pacemakers, surgical tools, diagnostics, and drug delivery systems.

The stock was at $87.47 on June 1, 2026. A year earlier it had been $139.

Three things happened in sequence to produce that decline:

First, Abbott announced a $21 billion acquisition of Exact Sciences in early 2025. Exact Sciences owns Cologuard -- the colon cancer test that people see advertised during football games, "no prep, no sedation, done at home." Growing at 30% per year. And it owns Cancerguard, a multi-cancer early detection test from a single blood draw. The market decided the price was too high and sold the stock.

Second, the flu season was weak. Abbott sells rapid flu, RSV, and COVID diagnostic tests. Fewer illnesses equal fewer tests. A temporary headwind treated by the market as a structural problem.

Third, Abbott lost a jury verdict in the baby formula litigation. $495 million. A large number, a known number, and once known -- already priced in.

None of these were permanent. The flu comes back. The lawsuit number is public. And the acquisition, despite the market's reaction, had put the fastest-growing cancer screening franchise in the country inside one of the most trusted healthcare brands in the world.

Meanwhile: the CEO spent $2 million of his own money buying Abbott stock at $107 per share. After the bad news. After the guidance cut. After the lawsuit. The CFO also bought. A board director bought 10,000 shares -- $927,000 -- on May 2. Three insiders, independently, wrote personal checks for shares at prices above where the stock traded on June 1.

Twenty-eight analysts cover Abbott. All twenty-eight rate it Buy or Strong Buy. The most pessimistic target: $106 from Leerink Partners. Our breakeven: $100.78. Even the bears were $5.22 above our breakeven.

The $100C Jul17 cost $0.78. Filled below the $0.95 ask on a limit order that caught a morning dip.

One contract. $78 at risk. July 16 earnings is the catalyst -- the first full quarter with Exact Sciences inside Abbott's results.

## GLOSSARY

**Cologuard:** Exact Sciences' flagship colon cancer screening test. Done entirely at home -- no colonoscopy prep, no sedation. Growing at roughly 30% per year. Abbott acquired Exact Sciences primarily to own this product.

**Cancerguard:** A multi-cancer early detection test from Exact Sciences that uses a single blood draw to screen for multiple cancer types simultaneously. Early commercial launch as of mid-2026. Targets a $60 billion addressable market.

**Cluster buy:** When multiple corporate insiders independently purchase shares on the open market with their own money, within a short window of time. CEO, CFO, and a director all buying within weeks of each other -- all at prices above the current market -- is considered one of the strongest bullish signals in public data.

**FreeStyle Libre:** Abbott's continuous glucose monitor. Worn on the upper arm, measures blood sugar every minute without finger sticks. The global market leader in its category and Abbott's fastest-growing product line as of 2026.

**NEC litigation:** Lawsuits alleging Abbott's Similac premature infant formula contributed to necrotizing enterocolitis in premature babies. A $495 million verdict was disclosed and is known -- already reflected in the stock price. Additional suits are possible but the surprise factor has been removed.

**Bear floor:** The lowest price target from Buy/Outperform analysts. Leerink Partners at $106 is the most pessimistic major analyst on Abbott. They are $5.22 above our $100.78 breakeven. Even if the bears are right, we make money -- that's what clean structure looks like.

**Exact Sciences acquisition:** Abbott bought Exact Sciences for approximately $21 billion, announced 2025, closed March 23, 2026. The July 16 earnings call is the first time Abbott reports with Exact Sciences fully inside the numbers -- three months of combined revenue, costs, and integration data.

**Expected value (EV):** A probability-weighted average of all possible outcomes. Calxter modeled five scenarios: at the probability-weighted average, this position has +146% expected return on the $78 invested. Positive EV does not mean it wins -- it means it would win on average over many repetitions. This one needs to win once.
