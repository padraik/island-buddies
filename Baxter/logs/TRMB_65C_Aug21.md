## GLOSSARY

**Bear floor:** The lowest price target held by analysts who currently rate the stock Buy, Outperform, or Strong Buy. Hold and Sell targets are excluded. If the bear floor is below the option breakeven, Rule 4 fails and the trade is dead regardless of other factors.

**Breakeven:** The stock price at which the position returns exactly $0. Equal to strike + premium paid. For this position: $65 + $0.38 = $65.38.

**Call option ($65C):** A contract giving the right to buy 100 shares of TRMB at $65 each. Gains value as the stock rises above $65. Worthless at expiry if the stock is at or below $65.

**Conviction score:** Internal rating of how strongly the Five Iron Rules case holds. 4/5 means all five rules pass and the thesis is solid but the required move (33.9%) is meaningful. 5/5 would require an obvious catalyst with tighter breakeven.

**Dot plot:** The Federal Reserve's summary of economic projections, specifically the grid showing where each Fed official expects interest rates to be at year-end. Released four times per year. A shift upward in median projections signals tighter monetary policy.

**EPS:** Earnings per share. Net income divided by share count. TRMB reported $0.42 for Q1 2026, up 56% year-over-year.

**FOMC:** Federal Open Market Committee. The Fed body that sets interest rate policy. Meets eight times per year. June 16-17, 2026 was Kevin Warsh's first meeting as Fed Chair; the outcome was hawkish.

**Hawkish:** A monetary policy posture favoring higher interest rates to combat inflation. The opposite of dovish. A hawkish FOMC outcome means rates stay elevated or go higher, which typically pressures rate-sensitive sectors including construction tech.

**Implied volatility (IV):** The market's estimate of how much a stock will move between now and option expiry, expressed as an annualized percentage. High IV makes options expensive. AI-disruption narrative stocks (INTU, CRM, HUBS) had IV so elevated that sub-$1.00 options only existed at 3x current price -- past every analyst target.

**Iron Rules:** The five screening criteria every position must pass before entry. See Five Iron Rules table in project_island_fund.md.

**OTM (out of the money):** An option whose strike is above the current stock price (for calls). A $65C is OTM when the stock is at $48.86. OTM options are cheaper but require a larger move to become profitable.

**Premium:** The price paid for an option contract. For TRMB: $0.38/share, $76 total (2 contracts x 100 shares x $0.38). The maximum loss equals the premium paid.

**Retail sell volume:** The percentage of trades placed by retail brokerage customers that are sell orders. Robinhood sell volume +34% vs prior day signals panic selling by non-institutional traders -- a potential contrarian entry signal when combined with strong institutional accumulation.

**SaaS transition:** Software-as-a-Service transition. Trimble has been converting from one-time hardware/license sales to recurring subscription revenue. Recurring revenue commands higher valuation multiples from analysts, which is part of the bull thesis.

**See-through risk:** The risk that the market prices past a good earnings quarter because it is already discounting a forward environment the quarter does not capture. For TRMB: if investors are pricing 2027 bookings softness (because Trimble's capital-intensive customers will delay contracts in a high-rate environment), a Q2 beat may not trigger the re-rating the trade requires. The stock can confirm the thesis and still not reach the breakeven within the option window.

**52-week low:** The lowest price a stock has traded in the past year. TRMB set a new 52-week low on June 17 at $48.86. Rule 1 requires the stock be near (within 20-25% of) its annual floor.
