## GLOSSARY

**BOTZ rule:** Named after a market observation about ETFs and themes. Themes without near-term data catalysts -- specific scheduled dates when new information forces market recalculation -- lose value steadily as time passes. Applied to options: if the catalyst has already fired and no new scheduled event exists before expiry, exit into remaining value rather than holding through decay.

**IV crush (implied volatility crush):** When a pending uncertainty event (earnings, FDA decision) resolves, the "uncertainty premium" built into option prices collapses immediately. Pre-earnings options are more expensive because the outcome is unknown. The moment earnings are released, IV falls and options become cheaper -- even if the stock moves up. Entering post-earnings, after the IV crush, pays for the same directional exposure at lower cost.

**Beat-then-fall:** A stock that reports earnings above analyst expectations (a "beat") but the stock price still declines, usually because forward guidance disappointed. Medtronic had done this multiple times in FY2026. The research correctly identified this risk as the reason to wait for post-earnings entry.

**MiniMed spin-off:** Medtronic's diabetes division (insulin pumps, continuous monitors) was being spun off as an independent company. The spin-off creates one-time accounting charges that depress reported earnings during the transition quarter. Not a sign of business deterioration -- a structural accounting event with a defined end point.

**Catalyst:** A specific scheduled event that forces market participants to reassess a stock's value. Earnings calls, FDA decisions, product launches, analyst days -- these are catalysts. Without one inside the option window, an option is held purely on the hope that value recognition happens spontaneously. It rarely does on schedule.

**Time decay (theta):** Options lose value every day simply from the passage of time, holding all else equal. The closer to expiry, the faster the decay. A position held for 44 days without a catalyst to counter the decay will likely see more of its value eroded by time than recovered by directional movement.
