## GLOSSARY

**Canna Cabana:** High Tide's retail cannabis brand in Canada. Approximately 180+ locations, making it the largest cannabis retail chain in Canada by store count.

**Gap-and-fade:** A market pattern where a stock opens significantly higher than the prior close (a "gap up") due to after-hours or pre-market trading on positive news, then falls during the regular trading session as sellers who had positioned for the spike exit their positions. Very common in small-cap stocks on earnings beats.

**GAAP net loss (vs. FCF positive):** High Tide shows a GAAP net loss because they are a roll-up company that grew through acquisitions. Each acquisition creates intangible assets (brand value, customer lists, licenses) that must be written down (amortized) on the income statement each year. This amortization is non-cash -- no money leaves the company. The actual cash business is positive: $18M in free cash flow.

**Exit rule:** A pre-set condition for closing a position. For HITI: sell all contracts at open the morning after earnings, regardless of the outcome. The rule exists to prevent the decision from being made in real time based on emotions or a stock price that may not hold.

**Roll-up:** A growth strategy where a company acquires multiple smaller competitors to build scale. High Tide has grown primarily by acquiring cannabis retail licenses and e-commerce accessory brands across North America.

**Autoship trigger (analog):** The specific metric that would confirm the thesis: recurring/subscription revenue growth year-over-year. For High Tide, this was the Canna Cabana membership and repeat order metrics. The thesis held -- revenue beat -- but the stock's microstructure at open did not cooperate.

**After-hours spike:** Trading in the period after market close (4 PM -- 8 PM EDT) when news is released. After-hours prices often gap significantly from the close but are based on limited volume. The gap does not always hold at the next regular-session open when full market liquidity arrives.
