# The Pre-Car Meeting
*June 9, 1996. Twenty minutes before Patrick picks him up.*

The bedroom is the same as it always is. Binder on the desk. Monitor on. The Robinhood app is open and there is a number on the screen that is making Bullxter physically unable to sit down.

Prime had called the meeting early. Before the car. That was unusual. Prime usually waited until after.

This time there were things that needed to be decided before Patrick heard any of them.

---

The number on the screen said: **+184%.**

Bullxter had been staring at it for four minutes.

"One hundred and eighty-four," he said. To no one in particular. To the room. "We paid forty-nine dollars. It is worth a hundred and thirty-nine dollars. The World Cup has not started yet. I want everyone in this room to sit with what I just said."

"I've been sitting with it," Bearxter said. "That's why I'm worried."

Bullxter turned around. "You're *worried*."

"I'm worried about Patrick."

---

Prime set his pen down. "That's why we're here. Walk me through it."

Bearxter's delivery was even, the way it always was. "Patrick is going to get in the car and he is going to see a hundred and thirty-nine dollars where forty-nine used to be. He is going to feel the way people feel when they are right about something they didn't fully trust. And the voice in his head is going to say: *take it.* Lock it in. Don't be greedy." He paused. "The voice is not irrational. Taking ninety dollars of profit on a forty-nine dollar bet is not a mistake. By most measures it is a completely sensible decision."

"It's also leaving two hundred dollars on the table," Bullxter said.

"*Potentially* leaving two hundred dollars on the table."

"Calxter," Prime said.

---

Calxter had been writing since before anyone else arrived. He looked up.

"Current state. DKNG stock is at twenty-seven-thirty. Strike is twenty-seven-fifty. We are twenty cents below the strike with twenty-three days to expiry. The option is pricing in elevated implied volatility because the World Cup opens in approximately forty-eight hours and the market is forward-looking." He looked at the page. "If the stock reaches thirty-one by expiry, the option carries intrinsic value of three-fifty. With remaining time value, the range is three-fifty to four-twenty depending on when it happens. That is a six-hundred-fourteen-percent return from entry."

Bullxter said "six-hundred-fourteen" out loud to no one, the way someone repeats a sentence they can't quite believe.

"However," Calxter continued. "CPI drops tomorrow morning at eight-thirty. The last print was three-point-eight. If May comes in at three-nine or above, risk assets re-price immediately. DKNG would not be immune. I am modeling a scenario where the option returns to the fifty-to-seventy-cent range in that event. That is still profit from our forty-nine-cent entry, but it is not ninety dollars."

"What's the probability CPI is hot?" Prime asked.

"Cleveland Fed nowcast has May at three-point-six. The distribution has a right tail. Forty percent chance in-line or below, forty percent chance slightly above, twenty percent chance materially above." Calxter set the page down. "Those are soft numbers."

---

Macxter spoke from the window. He'd been looking at his notebook.

"DKNG got an upgrade this morning. Analyst coverage -- I don't have the full note yet, but the price target is forty-nine dollars. That's the number driving the pre-market move. It's not World Cup anticipation alone. It's a fresh Buy with a forty-nine-dollar target on a twenty-seven-dollar stock." He paused. "That's a real signal. It's not me reading noise."

Bullxter made a sound that was somewhere between a word and a noise.

"Also," Macxter continued. "The administration signed an executive order this week directing federal agencies to cooperate with World Cup host city infrastructure planning. It is not directly related to DraftKings. It is the administration planting a flag in the tournament. No adverse posture signals on sports betting at the federal level. SAFER Banking Act has not been scheduled. Clean."

---

Bearxter: "None of that changes the CPI problem tomorrow morning."

Macxter: "No. It doesn't."

Bearxter: "So the question is whether Patrick can hold through a print that might give a portion of this back before the actual catalyst fires."

Silence.

"That's the meeting," Prime said. "Not whether to hold -- we're holding, the thesis is intact, the limit sell is at three-fifty, we're not moving it. The meeting is about how to present this to Patrick so he doesn't look at ninety dollars of profit the morning before CPI and make a decision he'll regret Wednesday when the World Cup opens."

---

Bullxter sat down. That meant he was thinking seriously.

"You show him the forty-nine," he said finally. "Not the ninety. You say: Patrick, we bet forty-nine dollars that DKNG would hit thirty-one before July 2. The stock is at twenty-seven-thirty. The bet is currently winning. But the bet isn't over yet. We haven't cleared the strike. We haven't seen a World Cup match. If we sell now, we are closing a winning bet before the game is played."

Bearxter: "And if CPI is hot tomorrow and it drops to seventy cents?"

Bullxter: "Then we sell at the floor. One dollar is the floor. We set it before he got in the car."

Bearxter, slowly: "The floor is the thing. If Patrick knows there is a floor -- that we cannot give all of it back, that there is a line below which we are out automatically -- then the ninety dollars doesn't feel like something that can disappear. It feels like something that is mostly protected with upside left." He paused. "That is the framing."

"That's not wrong," Prime said.

"I know," Bearxter said.

---

Calxter: "One additional note. The upgrade to forty-nine dollars implies the analyst believes DKNG is trading at roughly fifty-five percent of fair value. If they're directionally correct, the question of whether our July 2 expiry captures enough of the move becomes the operative constraint. We might be right about the direction and still expire before the full thesis plays out."

Bullxter: "That's the Sheldon argument."

Calxter: "Sheldon's August contract and our July contract are not in competition. They are different bets on the same direction. His captures the knockout rounds. Ours captures the opening week. One of us is better positioned. We will know by July 3."

Bearxter, very quietly: "Or both of us will be right and neither of us will admit the other had the better entry."

Bullxter looked at him.

Bearxter did not look back. He was writing something down.

---

Prime stood up.

"Here's what we tell Patrick. One: DKNG is up a hundred and eighty-four percent and the World Cup hasn't started. Two: the limit sell is at three-fifty, which is what the option is worth if the stock hits our original target. Three: the floor is a dollar -- if CPI is hot tomorrow and we fall below a dollar, we sell and we still double our money. Four: we have a forty-nine-dollar analyst price target that didn't exist yesterday."

He picked up the binder.

"Four data points. In that order. We don't ask him if he wants to sell. We give him the structure and we let him choose inside it." He looked at the room. "Patrick is not a trader. He is a smart man who wants to understand what he's in. Give him the structure and he holds. Give him the number alone and he takes it."

The room didn't argue.

Outside, somewhere down the street, a car engine turned over.
