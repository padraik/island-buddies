# MONDAY NIGHT
*June 1. Baxter's room. The binder is closed.*
*Four positions. Two pending. Thirty-four dollars in reserve.*

---

He opened Robinhood and looked at the pending orders. Two confirmation numbers. HITI and CCL, both fills at open.

He closed it.

There was nothing to research tonight. He'd known that since the CCL order went in. The fund had thirty-four dollars in reserve, four live positions, three different sectors, four different catalyst dates. The calendar was full. The research was done. Everything between now and Wednesday morning was waiting.

MDT reported Wednesday, pre-market. That was the only open question -- but "open question" was the wrong phrase. There was no question to research. There was only a number to watch. Either Medtronic held above seventy-six to seventy-eight after the print, or it didn't. If it held, he'd pull the July chain that afternoon. If it didn't, the watch entry cleared and he moved on.

Nothing to do about it tonight.

He pulled up MDT anyway.

Fifty-two-week range: fifty-five thirty-five to eighty-nine. Current price: seventy-four sixteen. Analyst consensus: one oh-seven. Fourteen buys, seven holds, two sells.

He already knew all of this.

He closed it.

The binder was open to the week-two section. He flipped to the back and found a blank page. In pencil -- he always used pencil in the back pages, ink in the front -- he wrote:

*MDT*
*June 3, pre-market*
*Hold above 76-78: pull July chain*
*Fail to hold: close the file*

That was the entire action item for the next forty-eight hours.

He looked at the page. Added:

*STZ -- spread was 56%. Thesis intact. Come back when the fund allows the $155 call.*

Not a loss. A deferral.

---

He went to look at the World Cup group draw.

Not because it changed anything. The NKE position was set. The exit rules were written. But he wanted to see how the schedule front-loaded -- which games were in which markets, whether the big group-stage draw games fell before or after June 30. Modelo was the number-one selling beer in the country. He was curious whether the schedule worked in Constellation's favor.

He looked at it for twenty minutes.

Then he put his phone down.

The schedule worked in Constellation's favor.

---

His fund table, written by hand:

| | |
|---|---|
| Total capital | $500.00 |
| NKE $50C Jul17 | $186.00 |
| ABT $100C Jul17 | $78.00 |
| HITI $2.50C Jul17 | $100.00 |
| CCL $31C Jul17 | $102.00 |
| **Deployed** | **$466.00** |
| Reserve (SGOV) | $34.00 |
| Distance to island | $4,999,500.00 |

The numbers did not change because he looked at them more. He knew that.

The next catalyst was June 15 -- HITI. Fourteen days. Then CCL on June 23. NKE and STZ both on June 30, which meant two prints on the same night, which was something he should have flagged earlier and hadn't. Macxter had caught it. He added a note: *June 30: two earnings same night. NKE and STZ. Watch for conflicting sentiment.*

Then he crossed out STZ. STZ wasn't in the fund. CCL was in the fund. CCL reported June 23.

He rewrote it: *June 30: NKE earnings. CCL already resolved. One print.*

That was cleaner.

---

He was asleep before two.

For Baxter, on a night with four active positions, two pending fills, and an earnings check in forty-eight hours, that was something like peace.

The binder was on the desk. Tab on week two. The island was still four million nine hundred ninety-nine thousand five hundred dollars away. The math was working.

He'd done what he could do today.

Tomorrow the orders would fill.
