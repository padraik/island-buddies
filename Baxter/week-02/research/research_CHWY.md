## GLOSSARY

**Autoship:** Chewy's subscription program where customers set up recurring deliveries of pet food, medicine, and supplies. Autoship customers have higher lifetime value, lower churn, and higher basket sizes than one-time buyers. Autoship revenue as a percentage of total revenue is the key metric that signals customer loyalty and business health. If Autoship is growing, the business is healthy even if the top-line headline misses.

**Autoship trigger:** The specific condition that validates the June 10 thesis: Autoship revenue higher year over year in Q1 FY2027 (ended May 2026) versus Q1 FY2026. If this condition is met, the Chewy thesis is intact and we hold through expiry. If it misses, we sell at open June 11.

**Forward PE:** Price-to-earnings ratio based on next year's estimated earnings rather than last year's actual earnings. CHWY's forward PE of 13.10 reflects analyst expectations for earnings recovery, even though trailing EPS is declining.

**Defensive category:** Industries where consumer spending is relatively stable even during economic downturns. Pet food is often cited as a defensive category because pet owners prioritize their animals' needs regardless of economic conditions.

**EPS decline (-42.9%):** Earnings per share fell nearly half year over year. This is the most concerning metric in the CHWY screen. Possible causes: investment in growth (distribution, pharmacy, vet), margin compression from competition, higher interest expense on debt. The June 10 earnings call should clarify the cause.
