## GLOSSARY

**Vault CRM Suite:** Veeva's proprietary customer relationship management platform built specifically for pharmaceutical sales and medical affairs teams. Formerly, Veeva's CRM ran on Salesforce infrastructure. The transition to Vault CRM is the key execution metric the market watches -- customers who complete the migration are fully on Veeva's own platform with higher switching costs.

**FCF margin:** Free cash flow divided by revenue. Veeva's 44% FCF margin means that for every $100 of revenue, $44 becomes free cash after all expenses and capital investment. This is an elite metric -- most software companies run 20-30% FCF margins. It signals the business generates substantial real cash, not just accounting income.

**Beat-then-fall:** When a company beats consensus earnings estimates but the stock declines anyway. Almost always driven by forward guidance that disappointed even though the past quarter was good. VEEV exhibited this tonight: Q1 revenue and EPS beat, but guidance for Q2 or FY27 came in below what the market expected, triggering selling.

**Vault:** Veeva's cloud content management platform used for regulatory document management, clinical operations, quality, and safety across the pharmaceutical development lifecycle.

**Life sciences SaaS:** Software-as-a-service sold specifically to pharmaceutical, biotech, and medical device companies. Characterized by long enterprise contracts, high switching costs, and regulatory-driven stickiness (customers can't easily migrate data from systems tied to FDA submissions).
