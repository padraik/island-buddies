## BEARXTER'S TAKE
*Written independently, before the meeting. Raw data only.*

There is a history here that needs to be on the table.

In February 2021, Wolfpack Research published a short report accusing EHang of fabricating revenue, using related-party transactions to simulate sales, and misrepresenting the status of regulatory approvals. EHang denied the allegations. The stock fell sixty percent in two weeks. A subsequent SEC investigation and short-selling pressure held the stock below twenty dollars for eighteen months.

I am not saying the fraud allegations were true. I am saying: this company has a documented history of investor skepticism about whether its reported numbers reflect reality. That history does not disappear because eleven analysts currently rate it a Buy.

The revenue numbers: FY2023 was 117 million CNY. FY2024 was 456 million CNY -- a 289% increase. FY2025 was 418 million CNY -- a decline. The massive jump in 2024 drove a lot of the analyst enthusiasm. The 2025 decline has not been explained in the public data I have. Declining revenue following a massive spike is a pattern that warrants a question, not an assumption of continued growth.

JPMorgan rated it Hold at $11. That is the only analyst who did not pile into the Strong Buy consensus. When one analyst breaks from ten, I pay attention to the dissenter.

The instrument: fifty cents for a Jun18 $10C that expires nine days after the earnings print. If earnings disappoint or the market is indifferent, there is very little time value left in a nine-day option to recover.

**Verdict going into the meeting:** CONDITIONAL APPROVAL -- I am not vetoing this. The bear target at $11 is above our break-even and that matters. But the fraud history, the revenue decline, and the widening losses are three things I want named before anyone says yes.

## GLOSSARY

**AAV (Autonomous Aerial Vehicle):** An aircraft that operates without a human pilot on board. EHang's EH216-S carries passengers and is fully autonomous -- no pilot, no remote operator. This is distinct from drones (cargo) and from human-piloted aircraft.

**CAAC (Civil Aviation Administration of China):** China's equivalent of the FAA. The CAAC type certificate EHang holds is the regulatory approval that allows the EH216-S to carry passengers commercially. This is the milestone that separates EHang from every other AAV company in the world.

**HFCAA (Holding Foreign Companies Accountable Act):** US law passed in 2020 requiring foreign companies listed on US exchanges to allow PCAOB audit inspections or face delisting. Previously applied to Chinese companies because China blocked audit access. A 2022 agreement between PCAOB and Chinese regulators substantially reduced the immediate delisting risk.

**Low-altitude economy:** A Chinese government policy designation covering commercial activity in airspace below 1000 meters. Includes autonomous aerial vehicles, drones, urban air mobility, and related infrastructure. Declared a national strategic priority in China's 14th Five-Year Plan with explicit financial and regulatory support.

**mNAV:** Not applicable to EHang (used in asset-holding companies like MSTR).

**PCAOB (Public Company Accounting Oversight Board):** US regulator that oversees auditors of public companies. The key risk for Chinese ADRs is whether PCAOB can inspect the auditors of Chinese-listed companies. As of 2022, access has been granted.

**Type certificate:** A regulatory document certifying that an aircraft design meets safety standards for commercial operation. EHang's type certificate from CAAC is the first ever issued for a passenger autonomous aerial vehicle globally.

**Wolfpack Research (2021 attack):** A short-selling firm that published a report in February 2021 accusing EHang of fabricating revenue and using related-party transactions to simulate sales. EHang denied all allegations. The stock fell 60% in two weeks. No criminal charges resulted. The incident created ongoing skepticism among some institutional investors about EHang's reported financials.
