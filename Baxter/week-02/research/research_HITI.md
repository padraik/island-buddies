## THE BAXTER CONVERSATION

*Late Sunday. Baxter's room. Binder open to a fresh section. This one came in at the end of a long day of screens.*

**BAXTER-PRIME**: "High Tide. Canada's largest cannabis retailer. Canna Cabana -- they have around a hundred and eighty stores. They also own the Daily High Club, Smoke Cartel, and Grasscity on the e-commerce side. Stock is at two forty-three. Fifty-two-week range is two-ten to four-oh-five. Near its lows."

**BULLXTER**: "Twenty percent of its range. Earnings in fourteen days. Six analysts at Strong Buy. The target is five-sixty-seven -- that's a hundred-and-thirty-one percent from here. What's the chain look like?"

**PRIME**: "Bid twenty cents, ask twenty-five cents. July seventeenth. Two-fifty strike. Twenty-five dollars at risk per contract."

*He set the chain down.*

"The spread is twenty percent. Wider than NKE or ABT. But at these dollar amounts, it's five dollars of friction per contract. If this works, that five dollars doesn't matter. If it doesn't work, we're down twenty-five. Not a hundred-and-eighty-six."

**BEARXTER**: "Walk me through the loss number. Because cannabis."

**PRIME**: "The reported net loss is thirty-four million on four hundred and sixty-four million in revenue. That sounds bad. Then you look at what's actually happening."

He opened the financials.

"Operating income trailing twelve months: plus seventeen million. Free cash flow trailing twelve months: plus eighteen million. EBITDA: plus forty-four million. The business is generating real cash. The GAAP net loss is driven by non-cash accounting -- amortization of acquired intangibles from their acquisition strategy. It's the same pattern we see in every roll-up that grows through buying assets."

**BEARXTER**: "Revenue is in Canadian dollars."

**PRIME**: "Six hundred and thirty million CAD. Roughly four-sixty USD. Growing seventeen percent year-over-year, which is an acceleration from seven percent the prior year. The cash business is getting better."

**CALXTER**: "The forward PE of forty-one is telling. For a company currently showing GAAP losses, a forward PE implies the market expects profitability. At two-forty-three per share and a forward PE of forty-one, analysts are modeling approximately six cents in earnings per share going forward. That's a near-breakeven inflection. If they show that trajectory on June fifteenth, the stock re-rates."

**BEARXTER**: "What's the sector doing."

**PRIME**: "Cannabis sector has been in a multi-year bear market in Canada. Legalization happened, then prices collapsed, then margins compressed as the market commoditized. High Tide's answer has been scale -- they're the largest retailer, which gives them buying power and brand recognition -- and their Cabana Club membership model, which gives them transaction data that smaller operators don't have."

**BEARXTER**: "Do we have a comparable? A time when a cannabis retailer near its lows, earnings approaching, re-rated on a clean quarter?"

**PRIME**: "Not a direct comparable. This sector doesn't have the same institutional playbook as healthcare or consumer brands. The analysts who cover it are specialists."

**BEARXTER**: "Then I want to know what the risk is if I'm wrong. What happens to the stock on a bad earnings?"

**PRIME**: "The stock falls toward the fifty-two-week low of two-ten. The option expires worthless. We lose twenty-five dollars per contract. On four contracts, we lose a hundred dollars."

*Bearxter was quiet for a moment.*

"That's the number I needed. A hundred dollars. That's the most that happens."

**BULLXTER**: "And if the quarter is good? Revenue continuing at seventeen percent growth, FCF positive, any improvement in operating margins?"

**CALXTER**: "At three dollars and fifty cents -- a forty-four percent move from today -- the option is worth one dollar. Four contracts return four hundred dollars on a hundred invested. At five dollars, the option is worth two-fifty. Four contracts return a thousand dollars on a hundred invested. At five sixty-seven consensus, the option is worth three-seventeen. Four contracts return twelve-seventy on a hundred."

*Silence.*

**PRIME**: "The math is asymmetric. The question is whether the thesis holds."

**BULLXTER**: "Six analysts. Strong Buy. Every single one of them has a target above five dollars. None of them are below our break-even of two-seventy-five. The most pessimistic informed opinion on this stock thinks it's worth double where it trades."

**PRIME**: "The order goes in tonight. Limit at twenty-five cents, four contracts, good for day. Fills tomorrow morning at open."

## BEAR CASE

What kills this trade:

1. **Revenue growth decelerates.** The 17% TTM growth slows materially -- new store openings flatten, same-store sales disappoint. Cannabis retail is still commoditizing in Canada.
2. **FCF turns negative.** If operating expenses increase faster than gross profit, the cash story that justifies the position breaks.
3. **Sector news.** Any negative regulatory development in Canadian cannabis -- pricing pressure, taxation changes, license freezes -- would hit the whole sector including HITI.
4. **No catalyst.** Management reports an in-line quarter with no evidence of the earnings inflection analysts are projecting. Stock stays flat or dips. Option expires worthless.

If things go wrong: stock falls toward $2.10. Option expires worthless. Four contracts, maximum loss $100. That is the absolute ceiling on what can go wrong.

## GLOSSARY

*Every acronym and referenced event in this file -- plain English.*

**Cabana Club:** High Tide's membership loyalty program for Canna Cabana cannabis retail stores. Members receive discounted prices in exchange for a monthly or annual fee. The program creates recurring revenue and generates transaction-level data on cannabis purchasing behavior -- a competitive advantage vs. independent dispensaries.

**CAD (Canadian dollar):** All of High Tide's financial statements are reported in Canadian dollars. At the current exchange rate of approximately $0.73 USD per CAD, $630M CAD revenue equals roughly $460M USD. All figures in "The Numbers" section are CAD unless marked otherwise.

**Canna Cabana:** High Tide's retail cannabis brand in Canada. Approximately 180+ store locations as of mid-2026, making it the largest cannabis retail chain in Canada by store count.

**Cannabis sector discount:** Cannabis companies in North America trade at significant discounts to equivalent consumer retail businesses because: (1) US federal illegality prevents many institutional investors from owning cannabis names regardless of the company's jurisdiction; (2) banking restrictions make capital raises more difficult and expensive; (3) many index funds and ETFs exclude cannabis names by mandate. This structural discount explains why HITI trades at 0.47x revenue despite growing 17% with positive FCF.

**Daily High Club / Smoke Cartel / Grasscity:** E-commerce brands owned by High Tide selling cannabis accessories (pipes, papers, vaporizers) in the US and internationally. These brands generate USD revenue, which partially offsets High Tide's CAD cost structure.

**EBITDA (Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization):** A measure of operating profitability that excludes non-cash charges. High Tide's EBITDA of $43.82M CAD TTM is positive -- the business generates cash at the operating level. The GAAP net loss is driven by items excluded from EBITDA, primarily amortization of acquired intangibles.

**FCF (Free Cash Flow):** Cash generated from operations minus capital expenditures. High Tide has generated positive FCF for three consecutive years ($14.88M in FY2023, $35.12M in FY2024, $17.87M in FY2025). Positive FCF means the business self-funds -- they are not burning cash to survive.

**Forward PE:** Price-to-earnings ratio using expected future earnings. A forward PE of 41.04 at $2.43 stock price implies analysts expect approximately $0.059 in EPS -- a small but positive profit. This would represent a material improvement from the current GAAP losses and signals an expected inflection toward profitability.

**Intangible amortization:** When High Tide acquires a cannabis retail license, e-commerce brand, or dispensary, they pay a price that includes brand value, customer relationships, and goodwill. Accounting rules require these "intangible assets" to be written down (amortized) on the income statement over time. This amortization is a non-cash expense -- no money leaves the company -- but it creates GAAP losses even when the business is generating real cash.

**Iron Rule:** The Island Fund's self-imposed rule limiting any single position to 50% of total fund value. At $500 fund, maximum $250 at risk. HITI at $100 = 20% of fund, well within the rule.

**Q2 FY2026:** High Tide's fiscal year ends October 31. Q2 FY2026 covers February through April 2026. Results to be reported June 15, 2026.

**Roll-up:** A growth strategy where a company acquires multiple smaller competitors to build scale. High Tide has grown primarily by acquiring cannabis retail licenses and e-commerce accessory brands. Roll-ups typically show GAAP losses during the acquisition phase due to amortization of acquired intangibles, even when the underlying business is cash-flow positive.

**Spread (bid-ask):** The difference between what a buyer will pay (bid $0.20) and what a seller will accept (ask $0.25) for an option. The $0.05 spread on the HITI $2.50C represents a 20% friction cost -- entering at $0.25 and immediately exiting at $0.20 would cost $5 per contract. For a position held through a 14-day catalyst, this friction is not meaningful.
