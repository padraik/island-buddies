## GLOSSARY

**Celsius Holdings:** US energy drink company headquartered in Boca Raton, Florida. Positioned as a fitness and active lifestyle beverage rather than a traditional energy drink. Distribution through PepsiCo's network. Listed on Nasdaq as CELH.

**Alani Nu:** Energy drink brand primarily marketed to women, with a fitness and wellness positioning. Known for flavors like Hawaiian Shaved Ice and Cherry Slush. Strong shelf presence in major retail (Walmart, Target, Costco). Acquired by Celsius in early 2026 for approximately $1.8 billion.

**Organic revenue growth:** Revenue growth from the existing business, excluding the contribution of an acquired company. Celsius's 138% total growth in Q1 2026 includes approximately two months of Alani Nu revenue. The 42% organic growth strips out Alani Nu and reflects how fast the base Celsius brand is growing on its own.

**PepsiCo distribution agreement:** Celsius signed a distribution partnership with PepsiCo in 2022, giving Celsius access to Pepsi's logistics and retail relationships across the US. In 2024-2025, the terms were renegotiated under tension -- Celsius wanted different margin sharing, Pepsi wanted exclusivity extensions. The terms were ultimately stabilized. The Q1 2026 organic growth confirms the channel is functional.

**Pre-earnings re-rating:** When a stock moves meaningfully before an earnings announcement, based on analyst commentary, operating data, or market sentiment rather than the earnings report itself. The CELH thesis is that nineteen Buy ratings and zero Sell ratings will begin driving institutional money into the stock in June-July, before the August 5 earnings show results.

**Implied volatility (IV):** The options market's estimate of how much a stock will move. CELH's IV of approximately 62% annualized is elevated, reflecting the uncertainty from the Alani Nu acquisition. Higher IV means the market expects larger moves, which makes options more expensive but also means break-even requires more movement.

**Bang Pharmaceuticals:** Formerly Bang Energy, a competing energy drink brand that filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy. Their market exit has reduced shelf competition for premium energy drink brands. Celsius specifically benefited from Bang's decline as retail space was redistributed.

**Q2 earnings (August 5, 2026):** The next time Celsius reports full quarterly results. This would be the first full quarter including Alani Nu since the acquisition closed February 2026. If Alani Nu integration is running on schedule, Q2 results would be the strongest validation of the acquisition thesis. This is the primary catalyst -- but it falls eighteen days after the July 17 option expiry.
