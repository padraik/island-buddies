## GLOSSARY

**Gross Bookings:** The total dollar value of all trips and orders processed through Uber's platform. Not the same as revenue (Uber keeps only a percentage of each booking). Growing bookings at 25% YoY while growing revenue at 14% means Uber is taking a smaller cut per booking -- intentionally, to drive volume during a growth phase.

**Uber One:** Uber's paid subscription program. Monthly fee in exchange for discounts on rides and Eats, plus priority access during high-demand periods. 50 million members represent approximately half of gross bookings, meaning subscribers account for a disproportionate share of volume.

**Autonomous Vehicle (AV):** Self-driving car. Uber is investing $10 billion to build a robotaxi fleet and partner with AV companies. The logic: if Uber doesn't have to pay drivers, margins recover dramatically. The investment period is now. The payoff is 3-7 years out.

**Non-GAAP EPS:** Earnings per share with certain non-cash or one-time items excluded. The "real" profitability measure most analysts use for tech companies where stock-based compensation and amortization distort GAAP numbers.

**Standard Deviation (SD):** In options math, a measure of how far a stock price is expected to move from current levels. One standard deviation means approximately 68% probability of landing within that range. Calxter calculated the $85 strike as approximately 1.5 standard deviations above current -- meaning random-walk probability of reaching it is roughly 7%.

**Positive Expected Value (EV):** When the probability-weighted average of all outcomes, including losses, is positive. A trade can be positive EV even with a low probability of profit if the payoff when it wins is large enough relative to the cost when it loses.

**FIFA World Cup 2026:** Held in the United States, Canada, and Mexico. 104 games, 16 host cities in the US, runs June 11 through July 19. Largest sporting event on Earth by viewership. Uber is one of the official mobility partners.
