## GLOSSARY

**Comparable sales (comp sales):** Revenue from restaurants open at least thirteen months. By stripping out the effect of new restaurant openings and closures, comp sales reveal whether the existing business is growing. A positive comp sales number means existing restaurants are selling more than they were in the same period last year. McDonald's US comp sales of +3.9% in Q1 2026 means the same restaurants are doing more business than a year ago.

**Systemwide sales:** Total revenue from all McDonald's restaurants globally, including both company-owned and franchised. Grew 11% in Q1 2026.

**McValue platform:** McDonald's US-focused value marketing program, launched to capture consumer attention in the current economic environment. Pairs with the Extra Value Meals relaunch. The +3.9% US comp sales growth in Q1 2026 is at least partially attributed to this program's success.

**Consumer trade-down:** When consumers under budget pressure shift spending to lower-cost alternatives. In restaurants, this means moving from sit-down dining to fast casual to fast food. McDonald's, as the value end of the restaurant spectrum, typically benefits from consumer trade-down environments. The bear thesis -- that consumer stress hurts McDonald's -- confuses correlation (consumers cut spending and McDonald's sells less) with causation (McDonald's is actually the beneficiary of spending cuts at higher-cost restaurants).

**Constant currency:** A financial presentation that removes the effect of currency exchange rate fluctuations. McDonald's earns revenue in local currencies around the world. When the dollar is strong, foreign earnings translate to fewer dollars even if the local business is growing. Adjusted EPS grew 1% on a constant currency basis in Q1 -- meaning the core business performance was modest. The weakness is currency, not operations.

**Franchised model:** Approximately 95% of McDonald's restaurants are franchised -- owned and operated by independent franchisees who pay royalties and rent to McDonald's corporate. This model provides McDonald's with relatively stable revenue (royalties on systemwide sales) with less direct exposure to restaurant-level cost inflation.

**Dow Jones Industrial Average (DJIA):** A price-weighted index of thirty large-cap US companies considered representative of the US economy. McDonald's is a Dow component. Institutional funds that track the Dow or hold large-cap consumer stocks own McDonald's. This creates a floor of institutional support below which the stock rarely stays for long.
