## THE BEARXTER DISCOVERY

*Bearxter had been watching LYFT for a long time. Not because he liked it -- he didn't. The bear thesis was too obvious. Waymo is coming. When it arrives, rideshare drivers become unnecessary and the Lyft business model becomes dependent on Waymo's price setting. Lyft has no pricing power in that world. Lyft becomes a commodity router. The margins compress to zero.*

*That was the thesis. He believed it. He's been watching LYFT fall from twenty-five dollars to fourteen while the thesis played out.*

*Then in late 2025, Lyft announced a partnership with Waymo for Nashville. Waymo would supply the vehicles. Lyft would manage the fleet and provide the booking infrastructure. Both apps would offer the rides.*

*Bearxter stopped.*

*"The bears were right that Waymo wins. They were wrong about what that means for Lyft."*

*Lyft doesn't need to own the cars. They need to own the demand. Twenty-eight-point-three million active riders in Q1 -- a Q1 record. Gross bookings up nineteen percent. Adjusted EBITDA up twenty-five percent. A positive net income of $14.2 million, versus $2.6 million a year ago.*

*The Waymo partnership is not a sign of weakness. It's Lyft inserting itself into the autonomous future as the booking layer -- the consumer-facing marketplace that routes autonomous vehicles to the people who need them. Waymo builds cars. Lyft has customers. That's a partnership, not a capitulation.*

*He brought it in.*

## GLOSSARY

**Gross Bookings:** The total value of all rides processed through the Lyft platform, including the fare paid by riders and driver earnings before Lyft takes its commission. Growing gross bookings at 19% YoY means there are more Lyft rides happening, at higher prices or higher frequency, than a year ago.

**Adjusted EBITDA:** Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization, adjusted for stock-based compensation and certain one-time items. Lyft's adjusted EBITDA of $132.8M in Q1 2026, up 25% YoY, represents the cash-generating capacity of the operating business. It is the metric the market uses to evaluate Lyft's path to sustained profitability.

**Active riders:** Monthly riders who took at least one Lyft ride. 28.3M active riders in Q1 2026 is a Q1 record -- more people using Lyft than at any previous first quarter in the company's history.

**Waymo:** Alphabet's (Google parent) autonomous vehicle subsidiary. Operates self-driving cars in Phoenix, San Francisco, Los Angeles, and Nashville. Has not completed a national rollout. The Lyft partnership: Waymo supplies the vehicles, Lyft manages the fleet and provides booking infrastructure in Nashville. Both apps can hail the same autonomous vehicles.

**Distribution layer:** In technology platforms, the entity that connects supply (products or services) with demand (buyers). Lyft's value in the Waymo partnership is not vehicle technology -- it's the 28.3 million active riders who use the Lyft app. In the autonomous future, whoever owns demand controls the margin. The Waymo partnership inserts Lyft as the demand layer for autonomous fleets.

**Nashville AV launch:** The Waymo-Lyft partnership in Nashville, Tennessee. First delivery of autonomous vehicles to Lyft's fleet in Nashville. Nashville is a significant market for ride-sharing (large entertainment district, airport volume) and a deliberate choice for the partnership's proof-of-concept.

**BofA Underperform at $14:** Bank of America's analyst rating on LYFT. An Underperform rating from a major bank at the current price creates a floor for institutional selling -- funds that trust BofA research may be unwilling to add positions until BofA upgrades. The $14 target implies they see flat returns. This is the key counterargument to Bearxter's 4/5 conviction.

**World Cup 2026:** Held in the United States. 104 games, 16 host cities. Runs June 11 through July 19, 2026. The same window as the Jul17 option. Lyft operates in most World Cup host cities. Airport rides, venue transport, and late-night rides from watch parties represent incremental volume.
