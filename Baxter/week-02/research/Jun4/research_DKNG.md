## GLOSSARY

**GAAP net income:** Under Generally Accepted Accounting Principles, the "bottom line" profit after all expenses, interest, taxes, depreciation, amortization, and non-cash charges. Companies often report "adjusted" or "non-GAAP" numbers that exclude items they consider non-recurring. A positive GAAP net income is harder to question than a positive adjusted figure. DraftKings' $21.1M GAAP net income in Q1 2026 means the company was profitable by the strictest accounting standard.

**Adjusted EBITDA guidance ($700-900M for 2026):** DraftKings' internal projection for their cash-based profitability metric for the full year 2026. This is the first year they've guided toward a $700M+ adjusted EBITDA figure. For context, the company was losing hundreds of millions of dollars per year as recently as 2022-2023.

**Sports betting handle:** The total amount wagered on sports through DraftKings' platform. More handle means more revenue (DraftKings keeps a percentage of each bet as the "hold"). The World Cup represents the largest global soccer betting event in history hosted in the US, and is projected to generate significant incremental handle.

**State-by-state licensing:** Online sports betting is regulated at the state level in the US. DraftKings operates in approximately twenty-five states that have legalized and licensed online sports wagering. Each state has different tax rates, responsible gambling requirements, and market structures. There is no federal online gambling law.

**Customer acquisition costs (CAC):** The marketing, promotion, and bonus spend required to acquire a new sports betting customer. The historical bear thesis on DKNG was that CAC was too high -- the company was spending $200+ to acquire customers who only generated $100 in lifetime value. The path to profitability required either reducing CAC or improving lifetime value. Q1 2026 GAAP profitability suggests the unit economics have improved.

**World Cup 2026:** FIFA World Cup hosted in the United States. 104 games, 16 host cities, June 11 -- July 19. DraftKings' first World Cup in a US-hosted, multi-city tournament environment with their current scale of twenty-five state licenses. The combination of the tournament's scale and DraftKings' geographic reach creates a material incremental handle opportunity in Q2 2026.

**FanDuel vs DraftKings:** The two dominant US online sports betting operators, respectively owned by Flutter Entertainment (FLUT) and DraftKings Inc. (DKNG). Combined, they control approximately sixty-five to seventy percent of US online sports betting handle. The competitive dynamic has been a race on promotions that historically compressed both companies' margins. As both have moved toward profitability, the promotional intensity has moderated.
