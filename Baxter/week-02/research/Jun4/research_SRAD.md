## GLOSSARY

**Sportradar Group AG:** Swiss-registered company, Nasdaq-listed (SRAD). Sells official sports data, odds feeds, and media technology to sportsbooks, broadcasters, and sports leagues worldwide. Not a sportsbook itself -- they sell to the sportsbooks.

**Data rights:** Official agreements with sports leagues (FIFA, NFL, NBA, etc.) that allow Sportradar to collect and resell real-time game data. Without official data rights, a data company can only sell unofficial data, which is less valuable and legally riskier.

**Short-seller report:** A research document published by an investor who has bet that a stock will fall. Motivation: if the report drives the stock down, the short position profits. Short-seller reports can be accurate, inaccurate, or selectively accurate. They are not filed with any regulator and are not subject to the same standards as analyst research.

**Class action investigation:** When a law firm announces they are investigating potential securities violations, they're recruiting plaintiffs -- investors who lost money -- to join a potential lawsuit. An investigation announcement is not a lawsuit, and a lawsuit is not a judgment. The sequence typically runs: short-seller report, price drop, law firm announcement, potential filing, potential settlement. Most class actions settle for less than the initial stock price impact.

**Implied volatility (IV):** The options market's estimate of how much a stock will move over a given period. SRAD's IV of approximately 45% annualized reflects the uncertainty from the April short-seller event. Higher IV means more expensive options -- and also more expected movement in either direction.

**EBITDA:** Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization. A cash-flow-adjacent profitability measure that strips out accounting charges. SRAD's adjusted EBITDA of €66M in Q1 represents real operating cash generation even though the GAAP net loss was €6M.

**FIFA data rights:** The official agreement granting Sportradar the right to collect and distribute real-time data from FIFA World Cup matches. This is the top of the data rights hierarchy -- official FIFA data is required for the highest-value sports betting markets (live in-game betting). Sportradar holds these rights for the 2026 World Cup.

**World Cup 2026:** Held in the United States, Canada, and Mexico. 104 games across 16 host cities in the US. Runs June 11 through July 19, 2026. Sportradar's internal research projected 62% of American soccer fans will bet on the tournament, with 29% placing their first ever sports bet.

**Q2 earnings (~August 2026):** Sportradar reports quarterly. Q1 2026 was reported May 20. Q2 covers April through June -- the World Cup quarter. If World Cup betting volume was as large as projected, Q2 revenue should show it. An August earnings report confirming strong Q2 numbers is the secondary catalyst for the Aug21 option.
