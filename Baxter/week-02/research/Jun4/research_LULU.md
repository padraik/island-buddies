## GLOSSARY

**Short interest:** The percentage of a company's outstanding shares that have been borrowed and sold short, representing bets that the stock will decline. High short interest creates pressure on the stock and, if the thesis is wrong, potential for a sharp reversal as short sellers buy back shares.

**Implied volatility (IV):** The options market's estimate of expected future price movement. When IV is high (as it is for LULU, reflecting the uncertainty around the brand and proxy fight), options cost more even for far out-of-the-money strikes.

**Proxy fight:** A campaign by a significant shareholder to influence corporate governance, typically by seeking board seats or pressuring management changes. Chip Wilson (LULU's founder) publicly criticized management's direction and retained advisors for a formal challenge. The campaign concluded without a board change, removing a persistent public narrative headwind.

**ASP compression:** Average Selling Price compression. When a brand's prices decline because of competition or promotional activity, the lower prices reduce revenue per item sold. If LULU's brand were truly deteriorating, ASPs would compress as they discounted to compete. There is no evidence of ASP compression in Q1 results.

**Guidance:** Management's public forecast for future revenue and/or earnings. Companies report actual results (what happened) and provide guidance (what they expect to happen). LULU beat Q1 actual results but lowered Q2 guidance. The debate: is the guidance realistic, or is management being conservative?

**Sandbagging:** When management deliberately issues guidance lower than their private expectation, creating the appearance of a "beat" in the next quarter. Not technically illegal but a gray area in investor relations. Post-crisis or post-proxy fight guidance tends to lean conservative.

**Q1 FY2026:** Lululemon's fiscal year runs February to January. Q1 FY2026 covers approximately February through April 2026, reported in early June 2026.
