## GLOSSARY

**FanDuel:** Flutter Entertainment's US sports betting platform. #1 market share in the United States for online sports wagering, competing primarily with DraftKings.

**EBITDA:** Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization. A profitability measure that strips out non-cash accounting charges. Flutter's EBITDA is growing even as GAAP net income declines, suggesting the underlying business is healthier than the headline number.

**GAAP:** Generally Accepted Accounting Principles. The required accounting standard for public companies. GAAP net income includes non-cash charges like amortization of acquired intangibles that can make a profitable business look like it's losing money.

**Amortization of intangibles:** When a company acquires another company, it records the value of brand names, customer lists, and technology as intangible assets. Those assets then "amortize" (decline in value on the books) over time, creating a recurring non-cash expense. Flutter has made many acquisitions, so this charge is large.

**IV (Implied Volatility):** A measure of how much the options market expects a stock to move. Higher IV means more expensive options. When IV is elevated heading into an event and the event passes without a big move, IV "crushes" and options lose value even if the stock is flat.

**Handle:** Total dollars wagered. Sports betting companies report handle as a measure of volume. FanDuel's World Cup handle would represent the total bets placed on World Cup games through the platform.

**STOCK Act:** Stop Trading on Congressional Knowledge Act. Requires members of Congress to disclose stock trades within 45 days. Macxter reads every filing.

**World Cup 2026:** FIFA World Cup hosted in the United States, Canada, and Mexico. 104 games across 16 host cities. Runs June 11 through July 19, 2026. First World Cup in the US since 1994. FanDuel's biggest international soccer betting event ever.
