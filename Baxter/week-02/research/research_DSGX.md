## GLOSSARY

**Global Trade Intelligence:** Descartes' data product that provides tariff classifications, duty rates, customs regulations, and trade agreement applicability across 200+ countries. Every new tariff schedule or trade policy change is new content that existing customers pay to access. Revenue is subscription-based and grows with trade policy complexity.

**M&A (Mergers and Acquisitions):** Descartes has grown historically by acquiring small, specialized logistics software companies and integrating them onto their platform. This is a compounding growth strategy -- each acquisition adds revenue, customer relationships, and data that makes the platform more valuable to all customers. "Well-capitalized" in the earnings release signals continued appetite for this strategy.

**Logistics software:** Technology that manages the movement and storage of goods through supply chains. Descartes focuses specifically on the compliance and visibility layer -- helping companies understand and comply with the rules that govern how goods cross borders, rather than the physical movement itself.

**FCF margin:** Free cash flow divided by revenue. DSGX's FCF margin of 35.7% means the business is highly cash-generative relative to its size, supporting both reinvestment and M&A without requiring debt or equity issuance.

**Bear-to-break-even gap:** The distance between the most pessimistic analyst's 12-month target and the options break-even price. The Island Fund requires this gap to be positive (bear above break-even). For DSGX pre-print, the gap was -$3.80 (bear below break-even). A bear revision to $88+ would create a positive gap of $2-3, making the entry viable.
