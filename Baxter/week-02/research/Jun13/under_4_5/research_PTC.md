## THE SETUP

The company has been growing revenue steadily and beat Q2 2026 EPS estimates by 28%. The stock has not noticed. The selloff has one origin: the market stopped believing the AI growth narrative and started asking when and where the next dollar of recurring revenue growth comes from. The answer lives in the July 29 Q3 earnings print. Until then, the stock sits at the floor and waits.

The play is not that PTC has solved its growth question. The play is that a 48% drawdown on a company that just beat estimates by 28% is overdone, the July 29 print is a live catalyst, and the Aug 21 call captures whatever rerating follows.

## THE THESIS

PTC spent the last eight years building an enterprise software franchise around digital manufacturing. PLM and CAD are not trends -- they are how aerospace and auto companies run product development. PTC's market position in industrial software is not under threat. The stock is down because the market bid it up on AI hype and then decided the hype had exceeded the revenue.

The growth concern is real but the magnitude of the selloff is not. PTC beat Q2 EPS estimates by 28%. Revenue exceeded expectations by 8.7%. Management explicitly flagged a "large, high-quality pipeline for the second half." The stock kept falling anyway because sentiment ran ahead of the data in both directions.

July 29 is when the data catches up. A Q3 print consistent with Q2 puts the stock somewhere between $135 and $160 on normal mean-reversion math. That does not reach the breakeven. A Q3 print with strong ARR growth and forward guidance that restores confidence in the growth trajectory puts the stock at $165-200 -- which does.

The instrument works only in the scenario where the catalyst fully resets the narrative. This is a binary bet on a sentiment reversal, not a grind-up thesis.

## GLOSSARY

**PLM (Product Lifecycle Management):** Software that manages how manufacturers develop, design, and bring products to market. PTC's Windchill and Arena platforms run PLM for companies like Lockheed Martin and Volkswagen. Not a trend -- a core infrastructure product.

**ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue):** The predictable subscription revenue PTC collects from customers on long-term contracts. ARR growth is the metric the market watches most closely for enterprise software. When ARR growth decelerates, stock multiples compress.

**Bear floor:** The lowest price target held by any analyst who still recommends buying the stock (Buy, Outperform, Overweight). For PTC, the lowest known Buy analyst is Barclays at $185. Rule 4 requires the option's breakeven ($165.70) to sit below this floor.

**Underweight (J.P. Morgan's rating):** Equivalent to Sell. J.P. Morgan expects PTC to underperform its peers or the market over the next 12 months. Their $162 target (above current price) means they think the stock can move but will lag. Their target is below the breakeven of $165.70.

**Iron Rule 2:** The earnings catalyst must fire before or within the options expiry window. PTC reports Q3 on July 29, 2026, which is 23 days before the Aug 21 expiry. Rule 2 passes with comfortable time.

**52-week range position:** How far the current price sits above the 52-week low, expressed as a percentage of the full high-to-low range. PTC at 5.4% is near the floor of its annual trading range.
