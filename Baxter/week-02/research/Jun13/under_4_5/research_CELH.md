## GLOSSARY

**Channel inventory glut:** An oversupply of product already sitting in distributor warehouses. Celsius's distributor (Pepsi) had too much Celsius on hand, so they stopped ordering new product. Revenue cratered while the product itself still sold. Once the warehouse clears, orders restart and revenue rebounds.

**Bear floor:** The lowest price target held by any analyst who still recommends buying the stock (Buy, Outperform, or Strong Buy rating). Used in Iron Rule 4 to confirm that even the most pessimistic bull thinks the stock will reach above the option's breakeven price.

**Iron Rule 4:** The option's breakeven price must be BELOW the bear floor. If the most pessimistic bull target is $44 and the breakeven is $41, we need less upside than even the bears expect. That is the structural requirement.

**Breakeven:** Strike price + option premium. The stock price at which the option starts generating profit. For the $40C at $1.00: breakeven = $41.00.

**52-week range position:** How far the current price sits above the 52-week low, expressed as a percentage of the full range. CELH at 4.8% is almost exactly at its annual floor.
