## GLOSSARY

**BSX:** Boston Scientific Corporation. Medical device manufacturer. Products include the WATCHMAN device (left atrial appendage closure for stroke prevention in AFib patients), electrophysiology catheters, and urology/endoscopy tools.

**WATCHMAN:** A FDA-cleared implantable device that closes the left atrial appendage in patients with non-valvular atrial fibrillation who cannot tolerate long-term blood thinners. BSX's flagship cardiology product. Volume decline in early 2026 triggered the April guidance cut.

**Electrophysiology (EP):** The cardiology subspecialty focused on electrical activity of the heart. EP procedures include catheter ablation for AFib. Competing ablation technologies (pulsed field ablation in particular) have increased pressure on WATCHMAN as an alternative to ablation rather than as a complement.

**Guidance cut:** On April 22, 2026, BSX revised full-year revenue growth from 10-11% to 7-8.5% and EPS to $3.34-3.41. Reason: WATCHMAN procedure volumes declining faster than expected, EP segment slowing, urology underperforming. Stock fell approximately 35% from prior levels.

**Left atrial appendage (LAA):** A small pouch in the heart where blood clots form in AFib patients. WATCHMAN seals it off to prevent clots from causing strokes. The LAA closure market is the primary battleground for WATCHMAN.

**Adjusted EPS:** Earnings per share excluding one-time items, stock-based compensation, and amortization of acquisition-related intangibles. BSX adjusted EPS of $0.80 in Q1 2026 reflects the underlying operating business performance.

**Iron Rule (instrument cost):** Island Fund rule requiring option ask ≤ $1.00 per contract (= ≤ $100 at risk per position). The $60C at $0.90 clears this ceiling. All strikes below $55 fail it.
