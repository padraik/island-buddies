## THE BEAR THESIS

CVNA's profitability recovery rests on two pillars: used car price normalization (after the post-COVID spike) and internal efficiency improvements (GPU expansion, reconditioning cost reduction). The bear thesis challenges the first pillar.

**The specific Q2 2026 disappointment scenario:**

1. **GPU compression from Manheim normalization:** The Manheim Used Vehicle Value Index tracks wholesale used car prices. In 2021-2022, the index spiked 50%+ as new car production collapsed (chip shortage). CVNA's GPU expanded during this period because it could sell used cars at inflated retail prices while acquiring inventory at slightly less-inflated wholesale prices. Post-2023, Manheim normalized. If Q2 2026 shows continued Manheim softness, CVNA's retail-to-wholesale spread compresses. The company is acquiring cars at the same prices but selling at lower retail prices.

2. **Volume outpacing reconditioning capacity:** CVNA scaled rapidly in the recovery. If it scaled faster than its IRC (inspection and reconditioning centers) can handle, per-unit reconditioning costs rise, directly compressing GPU. Management has cited reconditioning capacity as the key operational constraint. Any sign of operational strain shows up in GPU.

3. **Debt service as an earnings headwind:** The 2023 restructuring reduced total debt but at interest rates of 9-12%+ on the remaining notes. These interest payments are $400-500M+ annually. If EBITDA growth slows (GPU compression), the coverage ratio tightens. Analysts modeling the long-term thesis have priced in continued EBITDA expansion. Even a single quarter of flat EBITDA re-prices the multiple significantly.

4. **Rate environment effect on used car financing:** Higher rates (Warsh 3.5-3.75%) translate to higher monthly payments on used cars. Lower-income buyers — CVNA's core demographic — are most sensitive to monthly payment affordability. If origination volume softens because buyers can't afford the payments, unit growth misses.

**What the stock does:** CVNA is a high-multiple, high-expectation stock. A GPU miss of $50/unit (vs. consensus of $3,000+) combined with a volume miss of 5-10% could reprice the stock 25-35%. At $245 falling 30% = $171.50, below the $173.75 breakeven.

## GLOSSARY

**Gross profit per unit (GPU):** CVNA's primary profitability metric. Calculated as: (retail price received + financing income + add-on products) - (vehicle acquisition cost + reconditioning cost + transport). The absolute number is less important than the trajectory. Consensus expects GPU to keep expanding; any deceleration reprices the entire equity value.

**Manheim Used Vehicle Value Index:** Cox Automotive's monthly benchmark for wholesale used car prices. If the Manheim index declines, dealers (including CVNA) are paying less to acquire cars but also getting paid less at retail. The spread is what matters. Published monthly. A key leading indicator for GPU in CVNA's business model.

**Reconditioning (IRC):** CVNA's Inspection and Reconditioning Centers are the physical facilities where used cars are processed before sale. Each unit that goes through an IRC costs approximately $500-1,000+ in reconditioning labor and parts. As throughput scales, per-unit reconditioning cost should fall. If capacity constraints emerge, per-unit cost rises, directly compressing GPU.

**Beat-and-raise:** Colloquial for a quarter where the company exceeds both EPS consensus (the "beat") AND raises forward guidance (the "raise"). Critical distinction from the binder: simply beating EPS without raising guidance does not trigger the 4-quarter cap. Must be both.

**Short interest % of float:** The percentage of the company's tradeable shares (float) that are currently sold short. High SI (>10%) creates squeeze risk — if the stock drops, shorts may rush to cover (buy back shares), creating upward price pressure that works against a put thesis. Above 20% is extreme and makes directional options unreliable.

**GPU normalization risk:** The thesis that CVNA's GPU improvement was partly structural (scale improvements) and partly cyclical (unusual used car market conditions post-COVID). If the cyclical component reverses (Manheim softens, retail pricing normalizes), GPU growth decelerates or reverses. The valuation at $245 has priced in continued structural GPU improvement — cyclical headwinds break the model.

**Debt restructuring / covenant risk:** CVNA emerged from 2023 debt negotiations with ~$4.5B in notes at 9-12% coupon. These notes have maintenance covenants (EBITDA coverage ratios, leverage limits). A single bad quarter doesn't violate covenants, but it changes the refinancing math. The equity trades as if the debt is manageable; a GPU miss forces the market to recalculate that assumption.
