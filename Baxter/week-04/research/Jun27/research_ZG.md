## GLOSSARY

**Premier Agent:** Zillow's core business model. Real estate agents pay Zillow a subscription fee (or per-lead fee) to receive introductions from buyers browsing Zillow listings in their geographic market. When mortgage rates are high and transaction volume is low, fewer buyers are active on Zillow, fewer leads are generated, and agents either pause their subscriptions or pay less. Premier Agent revenue is therefore directly correlated with home transaction volume.

**Purchase mortgage origination:** A mortgage used to buy a new property (as opposed to a refinance, which replaces an existing mortgage). Purchase originations are volume-driven by buyers entering the market. With mortgage rates at 6.8-7.0% in 2026, many potential buyers are priced out or waiting. A rate reduction of even 50bps could unlock significant pent-up demand. Zillow's mortgage segment grew 56% YoY in Q1 2026 on purchase origination volume +96% -- the share gain indicates Zillow is capturing a higher share of the buyers who ARE in the market.

**MBA Weekly Mortgage Application Survey:** The Mortgage Bankers Association publishes a weekly index of mortgage applications filed with lenders. The Purchase Index (applications for mortgages to buy homes) is the leading indicator for housing transaction volume -- it typically leads closings by 6-8 weeks. A rising Purchase Index predicts rising Zillow revenue 6-8 weeks forward. Published every Wednesday morning.

**10-year Treasury yield:** The benchmark interest rate for the US economy. 30-year mortgage rates are priced roughly as 10-year Treasury yield + 1.5-2.5% spread. When the 10-year rises (as it has in the Warsh era), mortgage rates rise and housing affordability falls. When the 10-year falls (as it would on rate cut expectations), mortgage rates fall and housing demand returns.

**Rule 4 (calls):** The lowest Buy-rated analyst price target must be above the call breakeven (strike + premium). The logic: the most pessimistic professional who still rates the stock Buy must believe the stock reaches at least our breakeven level. A lowest Buy target below breakeven means we are betting the stock goes somewhere even the most pessimistic bull doesn't believe it will go. This is the structural discipline that prevents buying OTM calls on stocks where the analyst community has already reset their expectations.

**Hold analyst target vs. Buy analyst target:** For calls Rule 4, we count only BUY-rated analyst targets. A Hold analyst can have any price target -- it is irrelevant. A Hold analyst saying "target $40" means "I think the stock is fairly valued at $40 given current conditions" -- not "I believe this stock will return to $40." Only Buy analysts are making an active recommendation that the stock should trade higher.
