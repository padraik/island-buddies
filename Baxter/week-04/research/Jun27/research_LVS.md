## THE COMPANY

Las Vegas Sands Corp. operates luxury integrated resort casinos in two markets: Macau (China) and Singapore. Despite the name, no Las Vegas exposure -- the company sold its Nevada properties in 2021. Revenue is roughly split 55% Macau (The Venetian Macao, Four Seasons, Londoner, Parisian, Sands Macao), 40% Singapore (Marina Bay Sands), 5% other.

Macau is the world's largest gambling market by revenue. It was devastated by COVID lockdowns (2020-2022), then began a recovery in 2023-2024. LVS has the dominant multi-property footprint in Macau. Marina Bay Sands is the singular premium property in Singapore, which has near-monopoly status on casino gaming in the city-state.

Revenue drivers:
- VIP gaming: high-roller junket-organized play. Revenue per visit is enormous but highly volatile, regulated by Chinese authorities, and sensitive to high-net-worth confidence.
- Mass market: walk-in gambling from Chinese middle-class tourists. More stable, growing structurally as Macau diversifies away from VIP.
- Non-gaming: hotel, food and beverage, retail, convention -- growing as a % of revenue by design.

Q1 2026 results (reported April 22):

## GLOSSARY

**Integrated resort:** A casino complex that includes gaming plus hotel, entertainment, convention space, restaurants, and retail under one roof. LVS pioneered this format in Macau. The non-gaming revenue diversifies cash flow and allows properties to attract non-gambling tourists, which is important for Macau's government-mandated diversification policy.

**EBITDA (Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, Amortization):** The primary operating profitability metric for casino operators. Capital-intensive businesses (casinos cost billions to build) have large depreciation that distorts net income. EBITDA removes depreciation to show the underlying cash generation of operations. LVS's Macau EBITDA of $633M means Macau generated $633M in operating cash before building depreciation.

**VIP gaming:** High-roller casino play organized by junket operators who extend credit to wealthy players and collect a share of losses. VIP was 70%+ of Macau revenue in 2019 but has declined to 30-40% post-COVID as the government cracked down on junket operators and high-net-worth outflows. The Macau government and operators like LVS have been deliberately shifting toward mass market.

**Mass market gaming:** Walk-in table games and slot machines for regular tourists. Lower average bet but higher volume and more stable revenue. Not dependent on junket operators or high-net-worth confidence. LVS's mass market share of 25.7% in Q1 2026 is the highest it has reported since the recovery began.

**Concession:** Macau gaming license. There are six concessions total, held by six operators including LVS (through its subsidiary Sands China). Renewed for 10-year terms in 2023 after the government restructured the licensing system. Concession renewal was a key overhang that cleared in 2023.
