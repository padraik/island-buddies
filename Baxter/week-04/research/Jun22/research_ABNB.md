## BULLXTER'S TAKE

ABNB at $139 is priced on the assumption that short-term rental demand continues to outpace hotel supply. That was the 2021-2023 story and it was real. The question in Q2 2026 is whether that thesis has matured into a different story.

**The specific bear thesis for ABNB:**

Short-term rental growth is a two-variable model: nights booked × average daily rate (ADR). Airbnb's revenue = take rate × (nights × ADR).

Both variables are under pressure simultaneously:

**Nights booked growth slowing:** US domestic short-term rental demand is maturing. The pandemic travel boom pulled forward years of demand. Year-over-year growth comparisons are against a period of exceptional strength. If Q2 nights booked growth is below 5% YoY (vs. the 8-10% consensus), the "growth company" multiple becomes harder to justify.

**ADR compression:** Supply has expanded dramatically (more hosts listing properties) while demand growth is slowing. Price competition between hosts is intensifying. ADR flat or down YoY is the tell that supply-demand balance has shifted.

**Regulatory headwinds:** NYC's Local Law 18 (effective September 2023) required hosts to register and be physically present during stays. Similar laws are spreading to Los Angeles, Dallas, Denver. Each restriction reduces effective inventory in high-value urban markets — but also reduces bookings, because guests can't stay in unregistered apartments. Net effect on revenue: negative if urban market bookings fall faster than suburban/rural grow.

**If Q2 shows:** nights booked growth +4% YoY AND ADR flat YoY AND management guides Q3 nights growth at +3-5% — the multiple compresses from ~26x forward EBITDA to ~20x = stock reprices to $115-120.

That's our breakeven zone.

Bullxter verdict: The bear thesis is more diffuse than DASH but the required move is smaller (16.7% vs 25%). Both are viable. Portfolio correlated cap means we probably pick one, not both.

## BEARXTER'S TAKE

Let me push on the bear thesis.

**What breaks the ABNB bear case:**

1. **International mix shift.** If European and Latin American bookings are growing faster than US, the deceleration in US maturity is masked by international expansion. ABNB has been diversifying away from US dependence. Q2 might show international nights booked growing 20%+ even if US is flat.

2. **Experiences and non-traditional offerings.** ABNB has been developing "Experiences" (local tours, activities). If this segment contributes meaningful revenue growth, the ADR compression story on lodging is less damaging to total revenue.

3. **Supply constraint actually helps pricing.** If regulatory restrictions reduce supply (fewer hosts can list legally), and demand holds steady, ADR could actually increase in regulated markets. This is the bull paradox of regulatory headwinds.

4. **Brand pricing power.** Unlike food delivery (commodity-like), ABNB has genuine brand loyalty. Guests return to Airbnb specifically. Churn is lower than hotel alternatives. This argues against a multiple compression thesis.

**Bearxter's final concern:** The specific disappointment mechanism is less well-defined than DASH. DASH has a single measurable number: order volume YoY growth. ABNB has nights booked AND ADR AND mix effects AND international compensation. Multiple variables means multiple ways for the bear thesis to be partially wrong without triggering the full repricing.

## GLOSSARY

**Nights booked:** The core volume metric for Airbnb. The total number of nights reserved by guests through the platform in a quarter. Growth in this metric (YoY %) drives overall revenue growth alongside ADR.

**Average Daily Rate (ADR):** Average price paid per night across all Airbnb bookings in a period. ADR × Nights Booked = Gross Booking Value. A flat or declining ADR signals price competition between hosts (supply outpacing demand).

**Take rate:** Airbnb charges guests a service fee (typically 14-16% of booking subtotal) and charges hosts a commission (usually 3%). Total revenue divided by Gross Booking Value is the effective take rate.

**Multiple compression:** When a stock's price-to-earnings or price-to-revenue ratio decreases. For ABNB at 26x forward EBITDA: if EBITDA growth slows from 20% to 10%, investors might only pay 20x, causing the stock to decline even if EBITDA itself grows modestly.

**Local Law 18 (NYC):** New York City regulation effective September 2023 requiring short-term rental hosts to register with the city and be physically present during stays. Has dramatically reduced Airbnb listings in NYC, which was one of its highest-revenue markets globally.

**Correlated position cap (binder rule):** The Island Fund limits total exposure to any single primary macro driver to 35% of reserve. This prevents any one macro event (e.g., consumer spending collapse, interest rate spike) from devastating the entire fund simultaneously.

**IV crush:** The rapid decline in implied volatility (and therefore option premiums) that typically occurs immediately after an earnings report. If a stock stays flat after reporting, option premiums often fall 30-50% in one day as the "uncertainty premium" collapses. For put buyers, this means the stock must move significantly in your direction on the catalyst day to offset IV crush.
