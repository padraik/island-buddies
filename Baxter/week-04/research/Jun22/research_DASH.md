## GLOSSARY

**Gross Order Value (GOV):** The total dollar value of all orders placed on the DoorDash platform, including the food itself, delivery fees, service fees, and tips. This is the top-of-funnel metric. Revenue is taken as a percentage of GOV (take rate). GOV growth rate is the primary indicator of business momentum.

**Take rate:** The percentage of GOV that DoorDash keeps as revenue. Typically 13-15% for DASH. Higher take rates = more revenue per order but also more merchant unhappiness and potential loss of restaurant partners to Uber Eats.

**GAAP vs. adjusted EBITDA:** DoorDash reports positive "adjusted EBITDA" by excluding stock-based compensation, depreciation, and restructuring charges. GAAP net income is still negative. The bear case argues that SBC is real dilution to shareholders and true profitability is further away than the adjusted numbers suggest.

**Wolt:** European food delivery company acquired by DoorDash in 2022 for ~$8.1B in stock. Wolt operates in 25+ countries including Finland, Germany, and Israel. The acquisition thesis requires international growth to justify the price paid. Q2 international GOV is the key data point.

**Post-earnings theta decay:** After a binary event (earnings), implied volatility collapses rapidly, causing option premiums to fall even if the stock doesn't move (this is called "IV crush"). For a put buyer, this means: if DASH is flat post-earnings, the put might lose 30-40% of its value just from IV collapse. We need DASH to move our direction at earnings to overcome IV crush.

**Adjusted EBITDA margin:** DASH's target profitability metric (adjusted earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, divided by GOV). At ~4-5% adjusted EBITDA margin, the company barely covers capital expenditures. A margin guide-down below 4% signals the path to real profitability is lengthening.
