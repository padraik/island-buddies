**Inverted Iron Rules (puts version):**
- Rule 1: Stock in top 20-25% of 52-week range. No fresh 52-week high in prior 5 trading days.
- Rule 2: Confirmed earnings before Aug 21 expiry. Minimum 21 DTE at entry.
- Rule 3: Minimum 2 Sell/Underperform ratings. OR Buy-to-Sell downgrade within 30 days. (Buy-to-Hold does NOT count.)
- Rule 4: Highest Sell target must be BELOW put breakeven (strike - premium).
- Rule 5: Put ask at or below $1.50/share.

**Active disqualifiers (kill the play regardless of rules):**
- Short interest >10% of float → squeeze warning, requires minimum 4/5 conviction
- Any M&A rumors, strategic review, or active activist → automatic disqualifier
- 4+ consecutive beat-and-raise quarters → caps conviction at 3.5/5
- Catalyst not specifically expected to disappoint → caps conviction at 3.5/5

### CALXTER's Independent Pass (math only)

Calxter only cares about expected value. His verdict on each name:

**TSLA:** For a put to be worth entering, the probability-weighted return must exceed 0. For a $250P at $1.00 (38% OTM, 59 days):
- Breakeven: TSLA below $249 at Aug 21 expiry
- Estimated probability of TSLA <$249 in 59 days, given $405 start: ~8-12% (using historical TSLA 59-day distribution)
- Expected value: [0.10 * E(gain if <$249)] - [0.90 * $97] 
- Expected gain if below $249 (average landing ~$220): $250 - $220 - $1 = $29/share = $2,900 - $97 = $2,803
- EV ≈ 0.10 * $2,803 - 0.90 * $97 = $280 - $87 = +$193 on $97 at risk = +199% EV

Positive EV! But the probability estimate of 10% is uncertain. If the probability is 5%, EV goes negative.

**PLTR:** For a $75P at $1.50 (37% OTM, 59 days):
- Breakeven: PLTR below $73.50
- Probability of PLTR <$73.50 from $119 in 59 days: ~6-8%

## GLOSSARY

**Inverted Iron Rules:** The puts screening system derived from the calls system. Entry near 52-week HIGH instead of low; minimum 2 Sell ratings instead of zero; bull CEILING instead of bear floor; $1.50 ask cap instead of $1.00.

**Put breakeven:** For a put option: strike price minus premium paid. If you buy a $250P at $1.00, you break even when the stock is at $249. The stock must fall BELOW this to show a profit at expiry.

**Squeeze warning:** When a stock has >10% short interest, a positive surprise triggers short covering (shorts must buy back shares), driving the stock higher and making the put worthless. High short interest is a WARNING, not a positive signal for puts.

**Earnings history cap:** A company that has beaten AND raised guidance for 4+ consecutive quarters cannot exceed 3.5/5 conviction for puts. Their track record of positive surprises is structurally against the short thesis.

**Catalyst disappointment thesis:** Beyond rule 3 (analyst sell ratings), for a puts play to reach 4/5 conviction, the specific mechanism of disappointment must be documented. "Earnings is coming" is Rule 2. "Q2 margins will compress below 14% due to China price competition" is a catalyst disappointment thesis.

**BOTZ rule:** Themes without specific data mechanisms are dead money. Applies to puts: "this stock is expensive" is not a catalyst. "Q2 guidance will show a specific deterioration" is a catalyst.

**Put-call parity:** Mathematical relationship between call and put prices at the same strike: P = C + PV(K) - S. Used to estimate put prices from known call prices when direct put data is unavailable.
