## GLOSSARY

**SBC-adjusted profitability:** Stock-based compensation is a real cost to shareholders (dilution) but excluded from "adjusted" earnings. PLTR reports positive "adjusted" EPS but negative GAAP EPS in some periods. The AI/growth narrative ignores the dilution cost. Bears cite this to argue PLTR's profitability is partly illusory.

**10b5-1 selling plan:** A pre-arranged stock sale schedule where insiders commit to selling shares on a future schedule, legally insulating them from insider trading claims. PLTR CEO Karp has used these to sell shares systematically throughout the rally. This is technically insulated from the Form 4 insider signal analysis, but the direction (selling, not buying) at all price levels is bearish sentiment from management.

**Commercial vs. government revenue:** PLTR's government segment provides stable but lower-multiple revenue (US and international government contracts). The commercial segment (enterprise software sales) commands a higher multiple because it's theoretically more scalable. The bull case requires commercial to become dominant. Q2 commercial growth rate is the key number to watch.

**Beat-and-raise:** When a company reports EPS above consensus estimates AND raises guidance for future quarters above prior guidance. Four or more consecutive quarters of this pattern indicates strong earnings momentum that makes shorting structurally difficult.

**Short interest % of float:** The percentage of tradeable (float) shares that are currently sold short. Above 10% is the Island Fund's squeeze warning threshold. High short interest means many investors are betting against the stock; a positive surprise forces them to buy back shares (covering), amplifying any upward move.
