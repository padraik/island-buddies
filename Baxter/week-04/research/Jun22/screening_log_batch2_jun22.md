## GLOSSARY

**Handle growth:** In sports betting, "handle" refers to the total amount wagered. Handle growth YoY is the primary revenue driver for DraftKings. It differs from gross gaming revenue (GGR), which is handle × hold rate (the percentage the house keeps after paying winners). High handle growth with a stable hold rate = GGR growth.

**Net yield per passenger day:** Royal Caribbean's key operational metric. Total revenue minus commissions/transportation/other per available passenger cruise day. This is the primary driver of cruise earnings. If net yield per day is growing, the business is pricing well and costs are controlled.

**Hold rate:** In sports betting, the percentage of wagered dollars the sportsbook keeps as revenue (after paying winning bets). Typical hold rate is 7-9% on sports bets. A 1-point improvement in hold rate significantly impacts GGR with no change in marketing.

**Crypto transaction revenue:** Robinhood earns revenue when customers buy and sell cryptocurrency through its platform. In high crypto-volume periods (price volatility, retail FOMO), this revenue spikes. In low-volume periods, it collapses. This makes HOOD's earnings highly volatile and hard to predict.

**Payment for order flow (PFOF):** A practice where market makers pay brokerages for the right to execute customer trades. Robinhood built its "free trading" model on PFOF revenue. Regulatory risk from SEC attempting to ban PFOF is a background concern for HOOD's business model.

**Garena active users:** Sea Limited's gaming division (Garena) peaked in daily active users during COVID. Free Fire, its flagship game, drove billions in revenue. As pandemic-era engagement faded, Garena has seen secular DAU decline. Each quarter's Garena metrics are watched closely by bears who believe the gaming division is in permanent decline.

**Digital sportsbook market share:** In US online sports betting, FanDuel and DraftKings have approximately 70%+ combined market share. PENN's theScore, BetMGM, and others fight for the remainder. PENN has been losing share systematically as DKNG and FanDuel outspend on promotions and product quality. This structural share loss is the core bear thesis on PENN.
