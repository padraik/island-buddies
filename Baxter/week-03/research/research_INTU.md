## COMPANY OVERVIEW

Intuit runs TurboTax (consumer tax filing), QuickBooks (SMB accounting), and Credit Karma (financial products). The stock fell 65.5% from its $813 52-week high — erasing four years of gains — driven by two simultaneous threats: IRS Direct File expansion (free government alternative to TurboTax) and generative AI disruption concerns (ChatGPT as a tax prep tool). The company responded by cutting 17% of its workforce on May 20, 2026, the same night it reported Q3 FY2026.

## THE BEAR CASE

1. **Goldman Sell at $276**: Bears believe Intuit's moat is eroding. $276 is 2% below current price. If Goldman is right, the stock visits that level before our August 20 catalyst. Our option loses most of its value.

2. **17% workforce cut**: Management cut 17% of staff on the same night they beat earnings. That is not the action of a company confident in its growth trajectory. It signals either structural margin defense or existential concern about TurboTax's business model.

3. **IRS Direct File**: Expanded to 25 states. Free government filing directly competes with TurboTax for simple returns — Intuit's highest-margin segment.

4. **Sell-the-news established**: Q3 beat + guidance raise → stock slid. The market doesn't trust the numbers. Q4 on August 20 is off-peak (non-tax-season). Weaker Q4 numbers could extend the decline.

5. **AI threat to TurboTax**: ChatGPT and similar tools can answer basic tax questions. The "guided software magic" that made TurboTax irreplaceable is less irreplaceable with AI.

---

## THE BULL CASE

1. **Massive analyst divergence**: 28 Buy analysts at average $491 vs stock at $281. If the market is wrong about permanent TurboTax impairment, the recovery from $281 to $400+ is significant.

2. **QuickBooks is AI-resistant**: The SMB accounting segment (QuickBooks) doesn't face the same AI threat as consumer tax filing. SMBs need integrated payroll, invoicing, and compliance tools — not a chatbot. QuickBooks Online subscriber growth is the hidden story.

3. **Cost restructuring improves margin**: The 17% cut, if successful, materially improves operating leverage. If TurboTax holds even modest market share, the margin expansion is dramatic.

4. **Stock at technical support**: $268-280 is the 52-week low range. If the stock bounces from this level, $300+ is achievable before August 20.

5. **$0.70 for 7.1% breakeven**: The option is cheap relative to potential recovery. 93 days of time value.

## GLOSSARY

**TurboTax** — Intuit's consumer tax filing software. Primary revenue driver in Q2-Q3 (tax season: Jan-April). Revenue declining due to IRS Direct File competition and AI disruption concerns.

**QuickBooks Online** — Intuit's cloud-based SMB accounting platform. Subscription model. AI-resistant relative to TurboTax. Key growth metric: paid subscribers.

**IRS Direct File** — Free government tax filing program. Expanded to 25 states in 2025 tax season. Competes with TurboTax for simple returns only. Does not cover complex returns.

**FY2026/FY2027** — Intuit fiscal year ends July 31. Q4 FY2026 ends July 31, 2026; earnings August 20. Q4 is the non-tax-season quarter — QuickBooks is the primary revenue driver.

**Bear floor** — Lowest price target from Buy/Outperform/Strong Buy analysts only. Goldman Sachs Sell at $276 is excluded. Lowest confirmed Buy: Argus at $480.

**Sell-the-news** — Pattern where stock falls after reporting better-than-expected earnings. Observed in INTU Q3 FY2026 (May 20): beat + guidance raise → stock declined. Risk repeats August 20.

**ARR** — Annual recurring revenue. Key metric for QuickBooks Online subscription health.

**Fractional Kelly** — 1/4 Kelly position sizing. One contract ($70) is appropriate given 3.5/5 conviction and defined downside.
