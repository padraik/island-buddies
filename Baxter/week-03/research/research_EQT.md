### MACXTER — Geopolitical and Macro Assessment

*Cites filings, government sources, and macro events only. No earnings speculation.*

**The Iran Conflict and Deal — What Actually Happened:**

On February 28, 2026, the United States and Israel conducted strikes on Iranian nuclear sites, killing Supreme Leader Khamenei. Iran's countermeasure was to close the Strait of Hormuz.

The Strait of Hormuz is the most critical energy chokepoint in the world. It carries approximately 20% of global LNG supply (primarily from Qatar) and roughly 20% of global seaborne oil. Qatar produces approximately 77 million tonnes of LNG per year and is the world's largest LNG exporter. All of it was effectively blocked for approximately 3.5 months (late February through mid-June 2026).

This created an artificial scarcity in global LNG markets. US natural gas became comparatively more valuable as the alternative supply to blocked Qatari LNG. EQT's Q1 earnings (+94% revenue) reflect this artificially elevated pricing environment.

**The Deal:**

On June 14, 2026, a ceasefire agreement was reached, mediated by Pakistan. Terms include:
- US removes naval blockade
- Iran reopens the Strait of Hormuz
- Official signing ceremony in Switzerland — scheduled for "Friday" (estimated June 19-20, 2026)

**What happens next, in sequence:**

1. **Official signing (est. June 19-20):** Markets will price a second leg of energy price declines on confirmation.
2. **Strait physical reopening (days to weeks after signing):** Qatar tankers resume transit. Global LNG supply surges.
3. **Qatari LNG backlog clears (weeks):** 3.5 months of blocked LNG production enters global market simultaneously with resumed transit.
4. **Iran nuclear deal negotiations (months, uncertain):** The ceasefire is not a full nuclear deal. Iran's enrichment program is not resolved. Sanctions relief under a full deal could return Iranian condensate and pipeline gas to market — adding further supply.
5. **Global nat gas price normalization (months to 2H26):** European TTF and Asian spot LNG prices fall toward pre-conflict levels. Henry Hub, less exposed to global LNG, still declines as export economics weaken.

## GLOSSARY

**Henry Hub:** The main pricing benchmark for natural gas in the United States, located in Louisiana. Futures and spot contracts priced at Henry Hub serve as the reference price for most US natural gas transactions. At $2.77/MMBtu (post-Iran deal), prices are near lows not seen since before the conflict.

**Bcfe / Bcf/d:** Billion cubic feet equivalent / Billion cubic feet per day. Unit of natural gas production/sales volume. EQT produced 618 Bcfe in Q1 2026, equivalent to roughly 6.8 Bcf/day.

**MMBtu:** Million British thermal units. The standard energy unit for pricing natural gas. One MMBtu ≈ one thousand cubic feet (Mcf) of natural gas.

**LNG (Liquefied Natural Gas):** Natural gas cooled to -260°F for maritime transport. LNG allows landlocked gas producers like EQT (Appalachia) to access global markets via Gulf Coast export terminals.

**Strait of Hormuz:** Narrow waterway between Iran and Oman/UAE. Carries approximately 20% of global LNG supply (primarily from Qatar) and ~20% of seaborne oil. Closure by Iran in February 2026 was the primary macro driver of elevated energy prices that boosted EQT's Q1 numbers.

**TTF (Title Transfer Facility):** European natural gas benchmark price (Dutch hub). European prices are more directly affected by global LNG supply disruptions than Henry Hub. TTF fell 15%+ on the Iran deal announcement vs. Henry Hub's 3% decline, illustrating the US market's partial insulation.

**10b5-1 Plan:** SEC rule allowing corporate insiders to pre-schedule stock sales to avoid accusations of insider trading. Plans must be established when the insider is not in possession of material non-public information. EQT CEO Rice's plan was adopted March 6, 2026.

**Bcfe vs. Boe:** EQT measures production in Bcfe (natural gas equivalent), not barrels of oil equivalent (Boe), reflecting its pure-play natural gas identity.

**Bear floor:** In the Island Fund Iron Rules — the lowest price target held by any analyst with a Buy, Outperform, or Strong Buy rating. Hold and Sell analyst targets are excluded. EQT's bear floor is Barclays at $69.

**Fractional Kelly:** Risk management framework limiting bet size to a fraction (typically 1/4 to 1/2) of the Kelly Criterion's optimal bet, reducing volatility and drawdown. At 35-40% estimated win probability, Kelly math supported $40-50 deployment, not $69.
