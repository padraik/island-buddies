## GLOSSARY

**ABT:** The stock ticker for Abbott Laboratories, traded on the New York Stock Exchange.

**Baby formula lawsuit (NEC litigation):** Abbott's Similac premature infant formula was the subject of lawsuits alleging it contributed to necrotizing enterocolitis (NEC), a serious bowel disease in premature babies. In early 2026 Abbott lost a major case resulting in a $495 million judgment. This number is disclosed and known -- it's already in the stock price. Additional suits are possible but the surprise factor is gone.

**Break-even:** The stock price at which the option starts making money. For a $100 call that costs $0.78, break-even is $100.78. Below that price at expiry, the option expires worthless.

**Call option:** A contract giving the buyer the right (not the obligation) to purchase 100 shares of a stock at a fixed price (the strike) before a fixed date (the expiry). The buyer pays a premium upfront. If the stock never reaches the strike, the premium is the total loss -- nothing more.

**Cancerguard:** A product from Exact Sciences (now owned by Abbott) designed to detect multiple cancer types from a single blood draw. Early-stage commercial launch as of mid-2026. Targets a $60 billion addressable market in cancer early detection.

**CEO / CFO / Director:** CEO is Chief Executive Officer (runs the company day-to-day). CFO is Chief Financial Officer (manages finances and investor reporting). A Director is a member of the board of directors (oversees the CEO). When all three buy stock in the open market with their own money, it is called a cluster buy and is considered one of the strongest bullish signals available from public data.

**Cologuard:** Exact Sciences' main product. A colon cancer screening test done entirely at home -- no colonoscopy prep or sedation required. Growing at roughly 30% per year with no serious direct competition as of mid-2026. Abbott acquired Exact Sciences primarily for this asset plus Cancerguard.

**EPS (Earnings Per Share):** A company's net profit divided by its total shares outstanding. If a company earns $1 billion and has 500 million shares, EPS is $2.00. "Beating EPS" means the company earned more per share than Wall Street analysts predicted.

**EPS guidance cut:** A company formally telling investors to expect lower future earnings than previously stated. When Abbott announced the Exact Sciences acquisition, it simultaneously cut its EPS guidance because the deal adds debt costs and new shares, reducing per-share earnings in the near term.

**EV (Expected Value):** A probability-weighted average of all possible outcomes. Calxter assigns a probability to each scenario, multiplies it by the payoff in that scenario, and sums them up. Positive EV means the trade is profitable on average over many repetitions. It does not guarantee any single trade wins.

**Exact Sciences acquisition:** Abbott bought Exact Sciences for approximately $21 billion. Announced in early 2025, closed March 23, 2026. Exact Sciences is the company behind Cologuard and Cancerguard. The market reacted negatively to the acquisition price and dilution, dropping Abbott's stock by roughly $40. Abbott's CEO then spent $2 million of his own money buying the stock, signaling he believed the selloff was an overreaction.

**Flu season (diagnostics headwind):** Abbott manufactures rapid diagnostic tests for flu, RSV, and COVID. In years with lower-than-average illness rates, fewer tests are sold, depressing the diagnostics division's revenue. FY2025-2026 had a below-average flu season -- a temporary headwind that the market treated as a structural problem.

**FreeStyle Libre (Libre / CGM):** Abbott's continuous glucose monitor. A small sensor worn on the upper arm that measures blood sugar every minute without finger sticks. The global market leader in its category and Abbott's fastest-growing product line.

**Iron Rule:** The Island Fund's self-imposed trading constraints, written before any research began. The relevant rule here: no more than 50% of the fund in any single play. At $500, that means $250 maximum per position. $78 is well within this limit.

**Leerink Partners:** A healthcare-focused investment bank. Their $106 price target on ABT is the lowest of any major analyst covering the stock -- making it the most bearish credible estimate. Even at their pessimistic target, the stock would be above our $100.78 break-even. When Bearxter says "even Leerink is above our break-even," he means: we don't need optimism to be right. We just need the bears to be right.

**NEC (Necrotizing Enterocolitis):** A serious gastrointestinal disease that can affect premature newborns. Abbott's Similac premature formula was alleged in lawsuits to increase NEC risk. See "Baby formula lawsuit" above.

**Open-market purchase:** When an insider buys shares through a stock exchange like any regular investor, paying market price with personal funds. This is the most significant type of insider buying because it requires writing a personal check. It is distinct from stock grants (compensation), option exercises (often done at a deep discount), or 10b5-1 plans (scheduled purchases set up in advance).

**Probability of profit:** The estimated likelihood the option expires in the money -- worth more than what was paid. At 35%, this trade loses more often than it wins. The positive expected value comes from the wins paying far more than the losses cost.

**Q2 FY2026:** Abbott's second fiscal quarter of 2026, covering April through June. Reported on July 16, 2026. This is the first full quarter that includes Exact Sciences' revenue and costs inside Abbott's financials.

**RSV:** Respiratory syncytial virus. A common respiratory illness. Abbott makes RSV diagnostic tests. Like flu, RSV test volumes fluctuate by season -- a mild RSV season hurts Abbott's diagnostics revenue.

**Strike price:** The fixed price at which an option gives the right to buy shares. The $100 strike means the contract allows purchasing ABT at $100 per share. If ABT is trading at $108 at expiry, the option is worth $8 (the difference between $108 and $100, multiplied by 100 shares per contract).
