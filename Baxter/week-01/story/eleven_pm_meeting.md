# THE ELEVEN PM MEETING
*Monday night, June 1. Late. Baxter's room.*
*The binder is on the floor. There are four chairs and nobody's sleeping.*

---

## THE CALL

*Baxter had been going through the research for an hour when the phone rang. He picked it up on the first ring.*

"Yeah."

*Patrick's voice came through fast and high.*

"Baxter. My dad read the letter."

*The letter. The one Baxter had typed up from the weekend research — the_weekend.md, the thesis, the plans — and Patrick had printed it out and left it on the kitchen table.*

"What did he say."

"He said—" *Patrick laughed, the real kind, the kind Baxter had maybe heard three times in five years* "—he said 'this kid is going to lose all your money and yours too' and then he sat down and read the whole thing and then he asked me how much we'd need to make it worth doing and I said two hundred and he said 'what if I put in three hundred.'"

*Pause.*

"Baxter."

"Yeah."

"We might have five hundred dollars."

*Baxter looked at the wall for a moment.*

"Does he want in on the plays?"

"No. He said it's a gift. Birthday money from him too. He just — he liked the research. He said you sounded like you knew what you were doing."

*A long pause.*

"I'll call you before school."

"What does five hundred change?"

"Everything. Nothing. I don't know yet. I'll call you before school."

*He hung up.*

*The other three materialized in the way they always did — not suddenly, just present in a way they hadn't been a moment before.*

---

## THE MEETING

**BULLXTER** was already on his feet. He had been on his feet in some form for thirty-six hours.

"Five hundred dollars."

**BAXTER-PRIME** set the binder on the desk. Not on the floor. On the desk, open to the research summary page.

"Maybe five hundred. He said he *might* put in three hundred."

**BULLXTER**: "Let's work as if it's five hundred. Worst case we walk in tomorrow with the two-hundred plan and say 'if your dad comes through, here's what changes.' Best case we already know."

**BEARXTER** *(sitting, which he'd been doing more of since the Q4 meeting)* "What does five hundred change. Practically."

**BAXTER-PRIME**: "Iron Rule: max fifty percent per play. At two hundred, that's a hundred dollars. At five hundred, that's two hundred and fifty. Every play that was over limit at two hundred is now viable."

*He opened to the eliminated list.*

"Nike. The fifty-dollar call at a dollar eighty-four. At two hundred: a hundred and eighty-four dollars — ninety-two percent of the fund. Iron Rule violation. At five hundred: a hundred and eighty-four dollars — thirty-six-point-eight percent. Iron Rule compliant."

**BULLXTER**: "Nike is back."

**PRIME**: "Nike is back. With the full thesis we built all weekend."

**BEARXTER**: "We also said we don't have time to research plays that fit the new fund size. Everything on this table was researched at two hundred. We can't tonight invent new plays for five hundred that we haven't done the work on."

**PRIME**: "Agreed. We work with what we have. No new names."

**CALXTER** *(yellow pad, new page)*: "Let me map the plays against five hundred."

---

*He read aloud as he wrote.*

**At $500 — Available Plays Within Iron Rule ($250 max per position):**

|---|---|---|---|---|

| DKNG $30C Aug21 × 2 | $0.95 | $190 | 38% | ✅ |

| LYFT $17C Aug21 × 1 | $0.86 | $86 | 17.2% | ✅ |
| STZ $165C Jul10 × 1 | $1.45 | $145 | 29% | ✅ |

"At five hundred, the fund can support two simultaneous positions of up to two hundred and fifty dollars each — total deployed two hundred and fifty plus reserve in SGOV."

**BEARXTER**: "Iron Rule six. Sequential plays for first few trades. We've never made a trade yet. We have zero track record. The Rule still applies."

**CALXTER**: "Iron Rule six was written for a two-hundred dollar fund where one mistake ends the experiment. At five hundred, the reserve after two simultaneous positions at thirty percent each is two hundred dollars. That's more reserve than the entire original fund. The logic changes."

**BEARXTER**: "The logic of the rule doesn't change. The rule is about discipline, not math."

**BULLXTER**: "Nike and Abbott are different sectors, different earnings dates, different catalysts. Nike is June 30. Abbott is July 16. They don't even overlap. Running both isn't simultaneous exposure — it's sequential plays with different clocks."

*Silence.*

**PRIME**: "That's the argument I want to make. Make it again."

**BULLXTER**: "Nike earns June 30. The options expire July 17. By July 17, we know the result. Abbott earns July 16. The options also expire July 17. They close the same day. But the catalysts are separated by sixteen days. This isn't like running two bets on the same race. It's two different races with staggered start times."

**BEARXTER**: "And if both go wrong on the same day — July 16 — we've lost Nike's position AND we're underwater on Abbott going into earnings."

**BULLXTER**: "Nike resolves June 30. Not July 16. The position is open until July 17 but the catalyst happens June 30. If Nike goes well on June 30, we already know. Abbott's outcome is separate."

**PRIME**: "Calxter. If we run Nike and Abbott simultaneously at five hundred — what's the exposure?"

**CALXTER**: "NKE $50C at one-eighty-four plus ABT $100C at one-fifty — three contracts — total: three hundred and thirty-four dollars at risk. Sixty-six-point-eight percent of five hundred. Reserve: one hundred and sixty-six dollars plus SGOV on remainder."

**BEARXTER**: "Two hundred and twenty-five dollars if we hold the Iron Rule on each position individually. Run NKE at thirty-seven percent, ABT at thirty percent. That's three hundred and thirty-four. Sixty-seven percent combined."

"The Iron Rule says fifty percent on any *single* play. It doesn't address combined exposure on two plays."

**PRIME**: "So we're not technically violating the Rule. We're in the spirit question."

**BEARXTER**: "The spirit of the Rule is: don't blow up the fund. At sixty-seven percent combined, a complete wipeout on both is still sixty-seven percent loss. We still have thirty-three percent — a hundred and sixty-six dollars — to rebuild with."

**CALXTER**: "Expected value at five hundred with NKE plus ABT simultaneous: given four-out-of-five conviction on both, and independent outcome probabilities—"

*He wrote for ninety seconds.*

"The expected portfolio return on the combined position is significantly higher than the sequential plan. The added capital allows us to express both theses fully. The covariance between NKE and ABT is near zero — healthcare and athletic apparel have essentially no correlation."

**PRIME**: "Bearxter."

**BEARXTER**: "If we were at three hundred dollars of experience I'd say yes. We're at zero dollars of experience and we're considering running sixty-seven percent deployed on the first day."

*He sat with it for a moment.*

"Two plays. Four out of five on both. Sixteen days apart in catalyst timing. Different sectors. Different everything except the fund they come from."

*Another moment.*

"Tell Patrick at five hundred, we can run both. But we close the smaller one first if it wins early. No riding a winner while a second bet comes in."

**PRIME**: "That's the condition."

**BULLXTER**: "Then we have a plan."

## THE MATH ON THE DREAM

*Bullxter pulled a fresh piece of paper.*

"Let me do the island math real quick. If NKE works — stock goes to Goldman's $52, the $50C is worth two dollars — two contracts net a hundred thirty dollars. If ABT works — stock goes to analyst consensus $119, the $100C is worth nineteen dollars — two contracts net three thousand four hundred and fifty dollars. If DKNG works — stock goes to $37.52, the $30C is worth seven fifty-two — two contracts net one thousand three hundred and four dollars."

"If all three hit consensus targets: approximately five thousand two hundred and eighty dollars from a five-hundred-dollar fund."

*Quiet.*

**PRIME**: "That's ten-point-five times the fund."

**BULLXTER**: "In one summer."

**BEARXTER**: "The probability of all three hitting consensus in the same summer is—"

**CALXTER**: "Four-point-two percent."

**BEARXTER**: "Four percent."

**BULLXTER**: "But the expected value of the portfolio is still strongly positive even without all three hitting. Any one of these working changes the fund significantly. ABT alone at three contracts could return thirty-plus times the investment at consensus."

**PRIME**: "The island is a long game. We're not buying it this summer."

**BULLXTER**: "We're starting this summer. That's different from not trying."

*The room went quiet for a moment.*

**PRIME**: "Here's what we tell Patrick tomorrow. We tell him: we worked all weekend. We found two plays we believe in. If the fund is two hundred, we run them sequentially — ABT first, then DKNG. If the fund is five hundred, we run NKE and ABT simultaneously, then DKNG after they resolve. Either way, we're not guessing. We're making specific, researched bets with defined maximum losses and documented reasons."

"If his dad asks why he should trust a seventeen-year-old with three hundred dollars: show him the research. Show him that every play has a chain verification, an expected value model, and a Bearxter who tried to kill it."

**BEARXTER**: "The research is the pitch."

**PRIME**: "The research is the pitch."

---

## THE END OF THE NIGHT

*Baxter-Prime closed the binder. The other three were already gone — they always left without announcement.*

*It was twelve forty-seven AM.*

*He set the binder on the desk, the green cover facing up, and looked at it for a moment.*

*Two hundred dollars or five hundred. Sequential or simultaneous. Nike or not Nike.*

*The island was still the island. The math hadn't changed since eighth grade.*

*He turned off the desk lamp.*

*In the morning, he would go to Patrick's house, set the binder down, tap it twice, and say the thing.*

*The thing was ready.*
