# FUBOTV (FUBO) — RESEARCH FILE
*June 1, 1996. The broadcast rights play. Almost.*

---

## THE BAXTER CONVERSATION

**BAXTER-PRIME**: "FuboTV. Sports streaming service. They merged with Hulu + Live TV last year. The combined company has five-point-seven million subscribers. More importantly: they carry FOX and FS1 — which are the exclusive English-language broadcasters of the 2026 FIFA World Cup. All one hundred and four matches. If you want to watch the World Cup on a streaming service, you need Fubo."

*He taps the binder.*

"The stock is at ten dollars and sixteen cents. Fifty-two-week range: eight thirty-one to fifty-six sixty-four. The stock fell from fifty-six to ten in one year. EBITDA went from negative to positive — last quarter was up twenty-five hundred percent year-over-year. They just became a real business."

**BULLXTER**: "This is the one. World Cup starts June 11. Millions of people are going to want to watch it. They can't on Netflix. They can't on Disney Plus. They need FOX and FS1. That means they need Fubo. New subscriptions, new accounts, new revenue. The stock is near its lows and the catalyst is in ten days."

**BEARXTER** *(slowly)*: "When do they report earnings?"

**PRIME**: "Q2 FY2026 was reported May 6th."

**BEARXTER**: "Q2 ended when?"

**PRIME**: "Their fiscal Q2 ended March 31st — they're running on a September 30 fiscal year after the merger calendar change. So Q3 would cover April through June and report probably around August."

**BEARXTER**: "So the World Cup — which starts June 11 — is entirely in Q3. The Q3 earnings call would be in August or later. If we enter an option now, we're not playing an earnings catalyst. We're playing pure sentiment drift."

**PRIME**: "Yes."

**BULLXTER**: "But the drift is real. If Fubo starts reporting subscriber numbers — or if media covers the World Cup on Fubo — the stock moves before earnings."

**BEARXTER**: "Maybe. Or the market waits for actual numbers. And here's the other issue." *He looks at the chain.* "The option pricing. The liquid strikes — the ones with real volume — are June and early July. By the time we hit the July 2nd expiry, the chain shows the $12.50 call at seventy-five cents and the $13 at seventy cents. Break-even at $13.25 and $13.70. The stock needs to go from $10.16 to $13-14 just to break even. That's thirty percent."

**BULLXTER**: "If a million people subscribe to Fubo for the World Cup, the stock goes from ten to fifteen. That's not crazy."

**BEARXTER**: "It's not crazy. It's also not probable. Subscribers can watch for free if they have cable, or through Fox's free streaming, or through the library. Fubo doesn't have exclusive rights — Fox does. Fubo just carries Fox. The question is: do people subscribe to Fubo specifically to watch, or do they find another way?"

**BAXTER-PRIME**: "That's the question that ends this conversation. We can't answer it. And we need to be able to answer it before we put money behind it."

*He closes the binder.*

"No earnings catalyst, uncertain subscriber conversion, and the best options in the liquid part of the chain require a thirty percent move in thirty days. The thesis has imagination. The trade doesn't have structure."

## THE STRUCTURE PROBLEM

The World Cup (June 11 – July 19) is entirely within FUBO's Q3 FY2026. Q3 earnings report is estimated August-September. There is no option expiry between now and that earnings report that would capture the catalyst *and* give the thesis time to play out.

The pre-earnings options (June, July) are pure sentiment plays — betting on the market re-pricing Fubo before the data is confirmed. That's speculation without a floor.

## VERDICT

**FUBO: PASS — right position in range, wrong catalyst structure.**

The stock is genuinely near its lows after a massive decline. The World Cup broadcast connection is real and direct. EBITDA is turning positive. 

But without an earnings catalyst in the option window, this is a momentum bet without an anchor. The subscriber decline (-3.4% YoY) is a warning sign that the World Cup may not convert casual viewers to subscribers the way Bullxter hopes.

Watch it. If subscriber data leaks pre-earnings and the stock moves toward $13, the trade changes. Come back if the picture clarifies.
